The Legal Chain

Special Prosecutors · Attorney General · Suspension · Reinstatement

Three special prosecutors. One attorney general who met the mayor at a campaign stop and declined without public explanation. One suspension. One reinstatement. The formal removal petition against Mayor LC Jones moved through the legal system from January through May 2026 — and ended with a dismissal with prejudice on May 15, 2026.

The Record

Virginia Code 24.2-235 governs the removal of local elected officials from office. When a removal petition is filed, the Commonwealth's Attorney of the jurisdiction handles prosecution — unless a conflict exists, in which case a special prosecutor is appointed. In the case of Commonwealth v. Jones, Case CL26-011, the conflict question produced a chain of three prosecutors before the case reached trial.

The case alleged violations of the Virginia Conflict of Interests Act and bribery statute against Mayor LC Jones, based on the four undisclosed benefits documented in Chapter 2. It ended on May 15, 2026, dismissed with prejudice — before the Brown Edwards forensic audit was publicly released, and while the Virginia State Police investigation remained active.

What The Record Shows

The removal petition against Jones required three separate special prosecutors before reaching its conclusion. The case was dismissed with prejudice seventeen days after the third prosecutor was appointed — while the VSP investigation remained active and the forensic audit remained sealed.

The Attorney General — Declined Without Explanation

At some point in the prosecution chain, the matter was referred to the Virginia Attorney General's office. The AG declined to take on the prosecution. No public explanation was given.

The documented context: the Attorney General had met Mayor LC Jones during a campaign stop prior to the declination. The meeting is part of the documented record. The AG's office did not provide a stated reason for declining.

AG Declination — No Public Explanation

The Virginia Attorney General's office declined to prosecute CL26-011. No public explanation was provided. The AG had met Mayor Jones at a campaign event prior to the declination. These are the documented facts. The record does not state a reason — because no reason was publicly stated.

The Suspension — February 2026

In February 2026, Mayor LC Jones was suspended from office pending the jury trial in CL26-011. The suspension was a procedural consequence of the removal petition reaching the trial stage under Virginia Code 24.2-235.

What Parties Refused to Do

Commonwealth's Attorney Patrick Flinn noted in the case record that Jones and Ferrell-Benavides refused to cooperate with the investigation. Flinn stated he relied only on willing witnesses.

The subpoena list for CL26-011 includes: LC Jones, Nikima Robinson, Eric Payne, Karen Roberts, Richard Stanfield, Peyton Niblett, Robert Fincher, Kathy Lawson, Julian Mei, Rayshaun Gravely, and Aaron Rawls.

What The Record Shows

The two subjects of the removal petition refused to cooperate with the investigation. The prosecutor proceeded on willing witnesses only. Eleven individuals were subpoenaed to testify. The case was dismissed with prejudice before the jury heard the evidence.

The VSP Investigation

Separate from CL26-011, the Virginia State Police opened a criminal investigation into Martinsville governance matters. The dismissal of CL26-011 on May 15, 2026 did not close the VSP investigation. No charges have been announced. The investigation is ongoing as of this publication.

VSP Investigation — Active

The Virginia State Police investigation remains active as of April 21, 2026 — the last confirmed public documentation. The dismissal of CL26-011 did not close the VSP investigation. These are separate proceedings. No charges have been announced.
