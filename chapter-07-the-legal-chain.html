The Record

Virginia Code 24.2-235 governs the removal of local elected officials from office. When a removal petition is filed, the Commonwealth's Attorney of the jurisdiction handles prosecution — unless a conflict exists, in which case a special prosecutor is appointed. In the case of Commonwealth v. Jones, Case CL26-011, the conflict question produced a chain of three prosecutors before the case reached trial.

The case alleged violations of the Virginia Conflict of Interests Act and bribery statute against Mayor LC Jones, based on the four undisclosed benefits documented in Chapter 2. It ended on May 15, 2026, dismissed with prejudice — before the Brown Edwards forensic audit was publicly released, and while the Virginia State Police investigation remained active.
