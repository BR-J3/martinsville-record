Chapter Six

The Petition

Signature Drive · Three-Way Threshold Dispute · Judge Brinks Rules

In December 2025, Martinsville residents began a formal petition drive to remove Mayor LC Jones from office under Virginia law. Three officials cited three different signature thresholds. A judge ruled on which number was correct. The petition collected more than 375 valid signatures. It was not enough.

The Record

Virginia law permits citizens to petition for the removal of a local elected official from office. The process requires collecting a specified number of verified signatures from registered voters, submitting them to the Commonwealth's Attorney, and — if the CA certifies the petition meets legal standards — presenting it to the circuit court for a hearing.

In December 2025, a group of Martinsville residents began that process against Mayor LC Jones. They cited concerns about city governance and transparency. What became equally documented was the dispute over how many signatures they needed — a number that three different officials cited three different ways.

The Launch — December 23, 2025

Star News TV reported the petition drive launch on December 23, 2025. Patti Covington was identified as the group's principal administrator. The initial report cited the 375-signature threshold as the target.

What The Record Shows

Citizens organized a lawful removal petition based on a threshold provided by an electoral official. That number was wrong. The correct threshold — ruled by the circuit court — was 682. The petitioners collected more than 375 valid signatures. They fell short of 682. The petition did not advance to a court hearing.

Patti Covington — Beyond the Petition

Covington's role in this record does not end with the petition's outcome. She remained a documented public source on the underlying investigation afterward — it was Covington who publicly confirmed the Virginia State Police investigation remained active as of April 21, 2026, the same day Jones was reinstated to office. Note On Parallel Proceedings

The citizen signature petition and Commonwealth v. Jones (CL26-011) were separate legal proceedings running simultaneously. The citizen petition did not reach the threshold required to advance. CL26-011 was dismissed with prejudice May 15, 2026. Both proceedings are documented. Neither resulted in Jones's removal from office.
