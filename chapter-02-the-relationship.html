Chapter Two

The Relationship

Hot Tub · Airfare · Employment · Legal Advice

The Virginia State Police and the Brown Edwards forensic audit documented four undisclosed benefits flowing from a city manager to a sitting mayor. None appeared on any official disclosure form. Together, they form the factual foundation of Commonwealth v. Jones, Case CL26-011.

PeriodApril 2024 – Spring 2025
Primary SourceRawls Affidavit, Jan 12, 2026
Key WitnessEric Payne

StatusDismissed with prejudice May 15, 2026
The Record

From April 2024 through Spring 2025, City Manager Aretha Ferrell-Benavides provided Mayor LC Jones with four documented benefits that were never disclosed on his official Statement of Economic Interests: airfare for a personal trip, a hot tub delivered to his residence, an attempt to secure him employment at another city, and the arrangement of an attorney for his personal legal matter.

These are not allegations originating from a political opponent. They are facts documented in sworn court testimony, a notarized affidavit, subpoenaed records, and city financial documents — all of which formed the evidentiary basis of Commonwealth's Attorney Patrick Flinn's removal petition, Case CL26-011.

The Virginia Code standard governing removal from office includes misuse of office and neglect of duty. The Conflict of Interests Act — Virginia Code 2.2-31 — requires elected officials to disclose financial relationships. Both apply here. What follows is the documented record, in sequence.

1. Las Vegas Airfare — City of Martinsville Credit Card

On April 22, 2024, Aretha Ferrell-Benavides used a City of Martinsville government credit card to purchase airline tickets for Mayor LC Jones and his girlfriend, Nikima Robinson, for a personal trip to Las Vegas, Nevada.

Jones and Robinson departed May 18, 2024, and returned May 22, 2024. The charge to the city card: $544.71.

CH2-AIRFARE Las Vegas Airfare — Sequence of Events Apr–Jun 2024
  • Apr 22, 2024 — Ferrell-Benavides city credit card used to purchase airfare
  • May 18–22, 2024 — Jones and Robinson travel Las Vegas on city-purchased tickets
  • Jun 13, 2024 — Karen Roberts, Executive Assistant, emails Jones requesting reimbursement
  • Jun 25, 2024 — Robinson personally reimburses city $544.71 by personal check
  • Note: City was not reimbursed by Jones — reimbursed by Robinson, 64 days after purchase
Source: Rawls Sworn Affidavit, Jan 12, 2026 · CL26-011 subpoena records · Karen Roberts testimony

Karen Roberts served as Executive Assistant to the City Manager and Clerk of City Council. She was subpoenaed to testify in CL26-011. Her June 13 email to Jones requesting reimbursement is a contemporaneous record of the city's awareness that a personal trip had been charged to a government account.

What The Record Shows

Mayor Jones's personal airfare was purchased on a City of Martinsville government credit card. The city was not reimbursed by Jones. His girlfriend reimbursed it 64 days later. This benefit was not disclosed on Jones's 2024 or 2025 Statement of Economic Interests.

2. Hot Tub — Delivered to the Mayor's Residence

In September 2024, Eric Payne visited Ferrell-Benavides's residence. He observed a hot tub on the property and was told it was going to Mayor Jones. He was not, at that point, certain which hot tub was intended for the mayor.

On December 12, 2024, Payne visited Jones's residence. He observed a brand new, still-boxed hot tub that had been delivered there.

CH2-HOTTUB Hot Tub — Sequence of Events Sep–Dec 2024
  • Sep 2024 — Eric Payne visits Ferrell-Benavides residence, sees hot tub, told it's going to Jones
  • Dec 12, 2024 — Payne visits Jones residence, observes brand new boxed hot tub delivered
  • Dec 13–16, 2024 — Payne speaks to Ferrell-Benavides; she confirms she purchased a new hot tub rather than give her old one
  • Jones's response, July 2025: stated the hot tub was for his girlfriend, Nikima Robinson — told it didn't matter who received it
Source: Eric Payne testimony, CL26-011 · Rawls Sworn Affidavit, Jan 12, 2026

Eric Payne is a named witness, subpoenaed to testify in CL26-011. In Spring 2025, Ferrell-Benavides separately summoned Payne — who is not barred in Virginia — to advise Jones on an employment law matter involving his Henry County Sheriff's Department position. That interaction is documented under Benefit 4 below.

What The Record Shows

A city manager purchased a new hot tub and had it delivered to a mayor's residence. The city manager confirmed she bought a new unit rather than transfer her own. This benefit was not disclosed on Jones's 2025 or 2026 Statement of Economic Interests.

3. Employment Assistance — City of Petersburg

In Spring 2025, Aretha Ferrell-Benavides used prior professional connections from her tenure as Petersburg City Manager (2017–2021) in an attempt to secure LC Jones employment at the City of Petersburg.

This was not an isolated pattern. During her Martinsville tenure, Ferrell-Benavides had previously offered City of Petersburg Community Development Office positions to other Martinsville city staff — a documented use of prior employment connections as professional currency.

The Petersburg employment attempt on Jones's behalf occurred during the same period in which Jones voluntarily resigned from the Henry County Sheriff's Department, where he had served as a part-time deputy and school resource officer. Court documents indicate the resignation came amid a choice between resignation and termination. Jones resigned April 4, 2025.

CH2-EMPLOYMENT Employment Attempt — Documented Context Spring 2025
  • Spring 2025 — Ferrell-Benavides uses Petersburg connections to pursue Jones employment at City of Petersburg
  • Apr 4, 2025 — Jones resigns from Henry County Sheriff's Department (court documents indicate choice between resignation and termination)
  • Pattern: Ferrell-Benavides previously used Petersburg contacts to offer positions to other Martinsville staff
  • Jones's employment situation was active during the period in which his compensation negotiations were simultaneously under way
Source: Martinsville Research Hub / CL26-011 case record · Rawls Affidavit, Jan 12, 2026
What The Record Shows

During the same period when Jones authorized Ferrell-Benavides's pay raise in a closed session with no legal counsel present, she was simultaneously attempting to secure him personal employment using professional connections from a prior city. This benefit was not disclosed on his Statement of Economic Interests.

4. Legal Advice — Attorney Arranged for Personal Matter

In Spring 2025, Ferrell-Benavides arranged for Eric Payne — the same individual who later testified as an eyewitness to the hot tub delivery — to advise Jones on his employment law situation involving the Henry County Sheriff's Department. Payne is not barred in Virginia and provided non-legal observation only.

Separately, by October 2025, Jones had retained former Circuit Court Judge Verbena Askew as his personal attorney for a possible defamation suit against Aaron Rawls. In an October 22, 2025 email to City Attorney Steve Durbin and the full council, Jones disclosed that he and Ferrell-Benavides shared the same attorney — Judge Verbena Askew — and raised the question of whether this created a legal conflict.

CH2-LEGAL Attorney Arrangement — Key Facts Spring – Oct 2025
  • Spring 2025 — Ferrell-Benavides summons Payne to advise Jones on HCSD employment matter
  • Oct 2025 — Jones retains Judge Verbena Askew for potential defamation action against Rawls
  • Oct 22, 2025 — Jones emails Durbin and council confirming he and Ferrell-Benavides share attorney Verbena Askew
  • Jones raises conflict question himself — in writing, to city's own attorney
  • No formal conflict of interest challenge filed by city
Source: Oct 22, 2025 Jones email to Durbin and council · Martinsville Research Hub
Jones emailed City Attorney Steve Durbin and the full council on October 22, 2025, confirming that he and former City Manager Ferrell-Benavides were represented by the same attorney, former Judge Verbena Askew, and asking whether this created a legal conflict.
Source: Jones email, Oct 22, 2025 — confirmed in CL26-011 case record

Judge Verbena Askew: former Circuit Court Judge in Newport News, Virginia — the first Black woman to hold that role, appointed 1995. Her term was ended by the Virginia legislature in 2003. In 2010, a jury awarded $350,000 against her in a defamation suit; the Virginia Supreme Court upheld the verdict in 2012. She now operates Verbena Askew Law Firm PC in Hampton Roads. Jones hired her, notably, to pursue a defamation action — against a man she herself had lost a defamation judgment against as a litigant.

What The Record Shows

Ferrell-Benavides arranged legal consultation for Jones during an active employment dispute. Jones and Ferrell-Benavides subsequently shared personal legal representation through the same attorney — a fact Jones himself disclosed in writing to the city attorney. This arrangement was not disclosed on any official Statement of Economic Interests.

The Official Disclosure — What Was Filed

Virginia law requires elected officials to file an annual Statement of Economic Interests. The form includes Schedule G — the gifts section — which requires disclosure of gifts received above a certain threshold.

On both his 2025 and 2026 filings, LC Jones's Schedule G gifts section was blank. The "No" box was not checked. The table was completely empty. The sworn disclosure states no reportable gifts were received — during a period in which the airfare, hot tub, employment assistance, and legal consultation were all documented.

Statement of Economic Interests — Page 18, Schedule G

LC Jones's 2025 and 2026 Statements of Economic Interests, filed under oath, show the gifts section — Page 18, Schedule G — with the "No" box unchecked and the table completely blank. Filed during the period in which airfare ($544.71), a hot tub, employment assistance, and legal consultation were each provided by the city manager he supervised.

The Closed Session — March 17, 2025

On March 17, 2025, the Martinsville City Council met in closed session. No legal counsel was present. During that session, Jones was authorized to negotiate Ferrell-Benavides's compensation. He signed the offer letter — on official City of Martinsville letterhead — the following day. It took effect April 1, 2025.

The offer letter bears LC Jones's sole signature. No other council member co-signed. City Manager Robert Fincher later discovered the draft in Ferrell-Benavides's city email draft folder — the city was not formally notified of its existence until it was unearthed.

CH2-A March 18, 2025 Offer Letter — Confirmed In Hand Mar 18, 2025
  • Date: March 18, 2025
  • Signatory: LC Jones — sole signature, City of Martinsville official letterhead
  • Effect: 15% compensation increase, effective April 1, 2025
  • Closed session authorization: March 17, 2025 — no legal counsel present
  • Discovery: City Manager Fincher found draft in Ferrell-Benavides email draft folder
  • Rawls learned of it via news media on or about April 30, 2025 — six weeks after it took effect
Source: CH2-A Offer Letter (confirmed in hand) · Rawls Federal Complaint ¶26–27 · Fincher testimony, CL26-011

Council Member Aaron Rawls had been publicly opposing a pay raise for Ferrell-Benavides during the March 25, 2025 council meeting — eight days after the raise had already been signed, six weeks before he would learn that fact from a news report. The full record of that meeting is documented in Chapter 3.

What The Record Shows

Mayor Jones authorized and signed a 15% compensation increase for City Manager Ferrell-Benavides in a closed session with no legal counsel present, on city letterhead, under his sole signature — during the same period she was providing him with undisclosed personal benefits. The Virginia Conflict of Interests Act requires disclosure of any financial relationship that could reasonably be expected to influence an official's vote or action.

What The Case Found

Commonwealth's Attorney Patrick Flinn filed Case CL26-011 in 2026, citing Virginia Code 24.2-235 — the removal petition statute. The case alleged violations of the Conflict of Interests Act (Virginia Code 2.2-31) and bribery (Virginia Code 18.2-439), in addition to neglect of duty and misuse of office.

Flinn noted that parties — meaning Jones and Ferrell-Benavides — refused to cooperate with the investigation. He relied only on willing witnesses. Among those subpoenaed to testify: LC Jones, Nikima Robinson, Eric Payne, Karen Roberts, Richard Stanfield, Peyton Niblett, Robert Fincher, Kathy Lawson, Julian Mei, Rayshaun Gravely, and Aaron Rawls.

CL26-011 was dismissed with prejudice on May 15, 2026. Jones publicly called it total vindication. The forensic audit had not been publicly released at that date. The Virginia State Police investigation remained active. Two new federal lawsuits — Jones v. Rawls and Sands Anderson, and Ferrell-Benavides v. City of Martinsville, Rawls, and Sands Anderson — were filed 25 days later, on June 9, 2026.

The dismissal of a criminal or civil removal proceeding does not expunge the underlying facts from the public record. The documents exist. The subpoenas were issued. The witnesses were sworn. What follows in Chapters 3 through 10 is the sequence those facts produced.
