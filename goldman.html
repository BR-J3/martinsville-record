Paul Goldman

Attorney for Aretha Ferrell-Benavides; Adviser to LC Jones

A former chair of the Democratic Party of Virginia who has spent the last several years almost entirely on pro se activist litigation rather than representing clients in the traditional sense — and who showed up in Martinsville defending both the fired city manager and the suspended mayor at once. The question this record can't yet resolve isn't whether he's a real public figure; it's whether the "attorney" title he's used for decades actually carries a current Virginia license behind it.

Era One
The Richmond Career

Goldman chaired the Democratic Party of Virginia from 1990 to 1993. He worked on Robert F. Kennedy's 1968 presidential campaign, volunteered at Chicago's Cabrini-Green housing project, and began his legal career at the New Jersey Department of Consumer Protection in 1973 after earning a J.D. and M.P.A. from Syracuse University. He relocated to Virginia to work as a campaign consultant for Chuck Robb. In the decades since, he has run for office repeatedly and lost: a 2021 Democratic primary bid for Lieutenant Governor of Virginia, and a 2024 Richmond City Council race, both unsuccessful.

Goldman's more recent public profile centers almost entirely on pro se activist litigation — lawsuits he files and argues himself, as a voter or activist, rather than as counsel representing a client. His best-known recent case argued that Virginia's 2021 House of Delegates elections were unconstitutional for using outdated district maps, a suit he pursued largely alone over nearly a year before voluntarily considering dropping it. He has also formed activist entities like the anti-casino group "No Means No."

What The Record Shows

Multiple independent outlets — Wikipedia, Ballotpedia, the Roanoke Times, Virginia Mercury, RVA Magazine — consistently describe Goldman as an attorney, over a span of decades. That consistent description doesn't resolve the specific Virginia bar-licensing question raised in Martinsville; it does establish that Goldman is a genuine, long-standing public figure with a real activist and political record, not an obscure or fabricated identity.

Era Two
Martinsville
2025 – 2026

Goldman represented Aretha Ferrell-Benavides from at least September 2025, telling WSLS the city council had not provided proof of its claims against her and had withheld a copy of the investigative report. In October 2025, he announced intent to send the city a notice of claims citing 15 separate incidents between July 14 and September 8, 2025, estimating a possible lawsuit filing for late October or early November — a timeline that preceded the actual federal suit filed June 9, 2026 by several months.

"There is no crime. There is no fraud. There is no money missing. There is no bribery."
— Paul Goldman, on the Brown Edwards audit findings, April 2026

Goldman characterized the audit itself as having been "done on behalf of" the Sands Anderson law firm — his own framing of the same attribution ambiguity already documented in Chapter 5 of this record, where contemporaneous reporting blurred which firm actually conducted which workstream. In November 2025, with the city declining mediation on Ferrell-Benavides's EEOC complaint, Goldman said his side had been open to it.

By April 2026, Goldman's role had expanded: Cardinal News reported he was "also advising" Mayor LC Jones, simultaneous with representing Ferrell-Benavides, and had "questioned the apparent inconsistency of the law's application" regarding Jones's suspension.

Goldman represented or advised both of the two principal subjects of this entire record at the same time — Jones and Ferrell-Benavides — across the same period their own accounts of events (the hot tub, the raise, the audit) were sometimes in tension with each other on points of detail elsewhere in this record. Whether that dual role created any conflict between his two clients isn't addressed in any source reviewed.

Era Three
The Licensing Question

Aaron Rawls's sworn affidavit, notarized January 12, 2026, states he could find no evidence of Goldman being licensed to practice law in Virginia, despite Goldman representing himself as counsel in communications with media and city council.

Open Question — Now Better Contextualized

Goldman's well-documented public record fits a specific, consistent pattern: decades of press coverage describing him as an attorney, paired almost entirely with pro se activist litigation rather than traditional client representation. That pattern is exactly the kind of profile that could produce Rawls's specific concern — someone who has practiced law somewhere, or once held a license, or represents himself in his own name regularly, without currently holding the active Virginia State Bar membership his media presence implies. This record has not independently checked the Virginia State Bar's own attorney directory for Goldman's current status, and does not treat Rawls's affidavit or this page as a final answer either way.

What The Record Shows

Goldman is a real, well-documented public figure with a genuine four-decade record in Virginia politics and activist law. That record makes the specific Virginia-licensing question in Martinsville more precise, not less important: this isn't a question of whether Goldman exists or has any legal background at all — he clearly does — it's a narrower, still-unresolved question of whether his current Virginia bar status actually supports the "attorney" role he has held in this specific case.
