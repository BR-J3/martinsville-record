The Record

As of June 2026, five federal lawsuits involve Martinsville city governance figures. They span from June 2025 through June 2026. They name current and former elected officials, a fired city manager, a deputy sheriff, an interim city manager, and the law firm that conducted the city's own forensic investigation. The City of Martinsville — meaning its taxpayers — is a named defendant in at least one.

The cases are proceeding in the Western District of Virginia, Roanoke Division. Two docket numbers were pending PACER indexing as of June 11, 2026. The record will be updated as docket numbers are confirmed.

What The Record Shows

Five federal lawsuits. One city. The City of Martinsville is a named defendant in at least one case seeking $220,000 or more. Martinsville taxpayers are funding both the city's legal defense and the prior $500,000 forensic audit and $40,000 prosecution. Total exposure is not yet calculable. All five cases are active as of this publication.

The Taxpayer Exposure — Running Total

Every dollar spent defending these lawsuits, settling these lawsuits, or paying judgments in these lawsuits comes from Martinsville's city budget — meaning its taxpayers. The running documented costs as of this publication:

Documented Taxpayer Costs — Martinsville

Brown Edwards forensic audit: $500,000+. CL26-011 prosecution: ~$40,000. Ferrell-Benavides severance claim pending: $220,000+. Legal defense costs for five active federal cases: ongoing, not yet calculable. Total documented minimum exposure exceeds $760,000 — before any federal case reaches judgment or settlement.

This does not include any amounts the city may have paid in settlements, legal fees for individual defendants, or costs associated with the VSP investigation. The full financial picture will not be known until all five cases reach resolution.
