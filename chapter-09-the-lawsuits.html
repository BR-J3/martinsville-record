Three federal lawsuits arise directly from the governance crisis documented in Chapters 1 through 8: the council ejection, the firing, and the removal petition's fallout. Two additional federal cases — real, active, and unrelated to any of that — happen to name Robert Fincher and Aaron Rawls, two officials already central to this record, as defendants in separate matters entirely. The Three Core Cases

Case 1 — Rawls v. Ferrell-Benavides and Keen

Case 4:25-cv-00028 — Confirmed Docket
Aaron Rawls v. Aretha Ferrell-Benavides and Reva Keen
Filed: June 2, 2025 · WDVA Roanoke Division · 15 pages
  • Plaintiff: Aaron Rawls — Martinsville City Council Member
  • Defendants: Aretha Ferrell-Benavides (then-City Manager), Reva Keen (Deputy Sheriff)
  • Claims: civil rights violation — unlawful removal of elected official during protected speech
  • Basis: March 25, 2025 removal from council chambers — nonverbal signal, no verbal order
  • Complaint alleges Ferrell-Benavides and Keen "acted in concert" to infringe Rawls's free speech, citing video evidence of a nonverbal signal
  • Ferrell-Benavides's legal team has formally denied any such signal was given (response filed July 2025)
  • As of that filing, Keen had not yet filed a response
  • 15-page complaint confirmed in hand — full text reviewed

Case 2 — Jones v. Rawls and Sands Anderson

Filed June 9, 2026 — Docket Pending PACER
LC Jones v. Aaron Rawls and Sands Anderson
Filed: June 9, 2026 · WDVA Roanoke Division · 25 days after CL26-011 dismissal
  • Plaintiff: LC Jones — Mayor of Martinsville
  • Defendants: Aaron Rawls (Council Member), Sands Anderson (law firm)
  • Claims: civil rights violation of equal protection against Rawls and Sands Anderson; civil rights violation of First Amendment retaliation against Rawls; intentional infliction of emotional distress against Rawls; and defamation
  • The equal protection claim is grounded in alleged racial discrimination — Jones's suit alleges Rawls told him he would "make sure" fellow council member Rayshaun Gravely, who is Black, "would not receive a white vote in the City of Martinsville," and would "get out and go door to door" to that end. Rawls denies this.
  • This specific exchange is not solely a 2026 litigation claim — a Henry County Enterprise article from August 23, 2024 contemporaneously quotes both Jones and Rawls giving their respective accounts of the same conversation, nearly two years before this suit was filed. Full detail on the 2024 sourcing is documented on Gravely's and Rawls's person-pages.
  • Jones's suit alleges the bribery and corruption claims against him were "manufactured," and that the defendants then "fed that work product to the press, the Commonwealth's Attorney, and the court"
  • Jones's suit also characterizes the outside forensic audit as showing "no bribery or criminal wrongdoing" by him — his own description of a document not yet publicly released (see Chapter 5)
  • Filed 25 days after dismissal of CL26-011
  • Rawls, responding via Facebook, wrote he had not yet read the suit and blamed Jones and Ferrell-Benavides "for their own troubles," adding: "I strongly believe the healthiest outcome for Martinsville is to see this through to trial"
  • On the hot tub specifically, Rawls disputes Jones's account directly: "LC Jones is a liar"
  • Docket number pending PACER indexing as of June 11, 2026

Case 3 — Ferrell-Benavides v. City, Rawls, and Sands Anderson

Filed June 9, 2026 — Docket Pending PACER
Aretha Ferrell-Benavides v. City of Martinsville, Aaron Rawls, and Sands Anderson
Filed: June 9, 2026 · WDVA Roanoke Division · 25 days after CL26-011 dismissal
  • Plaintiff: Aretha Ferrell-Benavides — former City Manager
  • Defendants: City of Martinsville, Aaron Rawls, Sands Anderson
  • Claims against the city: First Amendment retaliation, Virginia Human Rights Act discrimination and retaliation, whistleblower retaliation, breach of contract, hostile work environment
  • Claims against Sands Anderson: contract interference
  • Claims against Rawls: defamation, intentional infliction of emotional distress
  • The discrimination claim alleges a Sands Anderson attorney asked her directly at the outset of its investigation whether she considered herself racist, and that the firm did not scrutinize white officials the same way. Rawls denies the underlying allegation and counters that Ferrell-Benavides "has been making racially-based decisions throughout her tenure."
  • Her suit also characterizes the outside forensic audit as showing "no bribery or criminal wrongdoing" by her — her own description of a document not yet publicly released (see Chapter 5)
  • Seeks compensatory and punitive damages, reinstatement, a hearing to clear her name, and "reasonable" attorneys' fees and costs — including the previously documented $220,000+ severance claim
  • March 18, 2026 — the U.S. Department of Justice's Civil Rights Division notified Ferrell-Benavides of her right to sue, a procedural step preceding this filing
  • City of Martinsville is a named defendant — direct taxpayer exposure
  • Filed same day as Jones v. Rawls — June 9, 2026
  • Docket number pending PACER indexing as of June 11, 2026

Both Suits Allege Race Discrimination — Both Sides Shown

Jones's and Ferrell-Benavides's suits each include a race-based claim against Rawls or the city. Jones alleges Rawls vowed to block a fellow Black council member from receiving "any white vote" in the city. Ferrell-Benavides alleges a Sands Anderson attorney asked her directly whether she considered herself racist, and that white officials weren't scrutinized the same way. Rawls denies both: on the hot tub specifically, "LC Jones is a liar"; on the broader pattern, Ferrell-Benavides "has been making racially-based decisions throughout her tenure." These are competing claims in active litigation, not adjudicated facts. Neither side's characterization is treated as established here.

Both June 9 suits point back to the same underlying dispute already documented in this record: both Jones and Ferrell-Benavides had separately questioned Sands Anderson's legal fees before the firm's investigation became the basis for the actions against them. Both lawsuits allege Sands Anderson increased its billing to the city by 245% in a single fiscal year, and claim that Jones and Ferrell-Benavides became targets of the firm's investigation specifically after they began questioning that increase and the firm's authority and conflicts in representing the city. Notably, Sands Anderson's report reached city council on July 22, 2025 — the same day council voted 3-2, with Julian Mei and Aaron Rawls dissenting, to finalize Ferrell-Benavides's salary increase from $183,500 to $215,000 — and the decision to use Sands Anderson for that investigation went against the Virginia Department of Risk Management's own advice to bring in outside counsel for a matter of that kind.

Watch This Space

Both suits claim the still-sealed Brown Edwards forensic audit shows "no bribery or criminal wrongdoing" by either Jones or Ferrell-Benavides. This is their own characterization of a document neither this record nor the public has seen in full — Chapter 5 holds until the audit's planned release, July 14–24, 2026. That release will be the actual test of this claim, not this lawsuit.

What The Record Shows

Three federal lawsuits trace directly to the events in this record: Rawls's original complaint over his removal from chambers, and the two suits Jones and Ferrell-Benavides each filed weeks after CL26-011's dismissal, both naming Rawls and Sands Anderson. The City of Martinsville is a direct defendant in one of the three, exposing taxpayers to Ferrell-Benavides's $220,000+ severance claim plus whatever damages or settlement may follow.

Two Separate Cases, Same Officials

Two more federal lawsuits name Robert Fincher and Aaron Rawls as defendants. Neither has any connection to the audit, Sands Anderson, or the removal petition — they are documented here because the same officials are involved, the same standard this record has already applied to the unrelated Timothy Howell case bundled with Krudys's other clients in Chapter 10.

Case 4 — Mook v. Hall

Case 4:23-cv-00028 — W.D. Va.
Daniel P. Mook v. Andy Hall and City of Martinsville
Filed: November 16, 2023 · WDVA Danville Division · Predates this record by two years
  • Plaintiff: Daniel P. Mook — former Assistant Commonwealth's Attorney in Andy Hall's office, employed April 3, 2018 to November 18, 2021
  • Defendants: Andy Hall, City of Martinsville
  • Subject: alleged FMLA (family leave) interference — Mook's 2021 request for family care leave, disputed certification, and termination on his last day of employment
  • June 5, 2025 — federal judge ruled Hall is not entitled to qualified immunity; case proceeds to a jury on disputed facts
  • No trial date currently confirmed — an earlier March 2025 trial setting was superseded by Hall's pending summary judgment motion
  • Entirely unrelated to the bribery/conflict-of-interest allegations against Jones, the audit, or CL26-011
  • Full detail on Hall's role is documented on his person-page

Case 5 — Johnson v. Fincher and Rawls

Docket Pending PACER
Rico Johnson v. Robert Fincher and Aaron Rawls
Filed: 2025 · Martinsville Bulletin reported Oct 12, 2025 · Seeks $2 million
  • Plaintiff: Rico Johnson, former owner of the Kornna Kitchen restaurant, uptown Martinsville
  • Defendants: Robert Fincher, Aaron Rawls
  • Subject: defamation — Johnson alleges comments by Fincher and Rawls caused "injury and harm to both their good personal reputation and their good business reputation," along with "humiliation, shame, vilification, exposure to public infamy, scandal, and disgrace"
  • Underlying context: Johnson had been charged with nine felony counts of obtaining money by false pretense and nine felony counts of larceny by bad check, alleging insufficient funds exceeding $40,000 owed to food vendors; certified to circuit court June 12, indicted by grand jury July 28, pleaded not guilty at arraignment
  • Fincher's comments concerned his role as police chief specifically — Fincher stated: "all actions and allegations concern my actions as the police chief... truth is a complete defense against defamation"
  • Rawls's comments, made publicly after Johnson's indictment, included: "Rico... ruined his own business. It's time for him to act his age and take accountability for his bad behavior"
  • Entirely unrelated to the governance crisis, the audit, or CL26-011
  • Full record — including the cooperative pre-arrest years, the council statement on race, and the Danville relaunch — is documented on Johnson's person-page

What The Record Shows

Mook v. Hall and Johnson v. Fincher and Rawls are both real, active federal cases. Neither belongs in a count of lawsuits "about" the Martinsville governance crisis — one is a 2023 family-leave dispute predating everything else in this record, the other a defamation claim over a restaurant fraud case with no connection to the audit or the removal petition. They are documented here, separately and precisely, because the officials overlap. The overlap is the only thing connecting them to the rest of this record.

The Taxpayer Exposure — Running Total

Every dollar spent defending these lawsuits, settling them, or paying judgments in them comes from Martinsville's city budget — meaning its taxpayers. The running documented costs as of this publication, from the three core cases plus the underlying investigation already detailed in this record:

Documented Taxpayer Costs — Martinsville

Brown Edwards forensic audit: $500,000+. CL26-011 prosecution: ~$40,000. Ferrell-Benavides severance claim pending: $220,000+. Legal defense costs for the three core federal cases: ongoing, not yet calculable. Total documented minimum exposure exceeds $760,000 — before any of the three core cases reaches judgment or settlement. This figure does not include Mook v. Hall or Johnson v. Fincher and Rawls, which are separate matters; any city exposure from those cases is not yet known and is tracked independently.

This does not include any amounts the city may have paid in settlements, legal fees for individual defendants, or costs associated with the VSP investigation. The full financial picture will not be known until all three core cases reach resolution.
