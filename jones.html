LC Jones

Mayor, City of Martinsville (at-large council seat) — 2022–Present

An unauthorized raise, a council member removed on a nonverbal signal, a year-long legal fight that ended in dismissal — and an attorney with a statewide reputation for suing the very institutions Jones now leans on. This page pulls together everything on the public record about Jones, across the full arc of this case.

Era One
The Conduct
2024 – 2025

Full documentation of the underlying conduct — the four undisclosed benefits, the closed-session raise, the March 25 ejection — is covered in Chapters 1 through 4. This page focuses on what those chapters establish about Jones specifically, plus material that sits outside the chapter narrative.

The Closed-Session Raise and the Recusal Refusal

March 17, 2025: closed session, no legal counsel present, Jones authorized to negotiate Ferrell-Benavides's compensation. March 18: Jones signs the offer letter alone — sole signature on city letterhead.

What The Record Shows

Separately, and not yet detailed in any chapter: Jones refused repeated requests from Sands Anderson attorney Steve Durbin to recuse himself from closed-session matters where he had an undisclosed conflict of interest, despite objections from city legal counsel and other council members. He also failed to disclose his conflict of interest on his Statement of Economic Interests, as required under Virginia Code § 2.2-3115 — a precise statutory citation, not just a general disclosure failure.

The March 25 Ejection

Full detail in Chapter 3: Jones told Rawls to "fall in order," gaveled while Rawls was speaking, accused him of "making personal attacks," and the removal followed. Jones's own role in directing the meeting — not just Ferrell-Benavides's nod — is the documented center of that chapter.

The Roark Irony

In June 2024 — a year before his own conduct came under formal investigation — Jones publicly attacked his own Police Chief, Robert Fincher, over the department's handling of a Charles Roark assault case, telling the Martinsville Bulletin: "The Chief is just stepping back, washing his hands and hoping it goes away!" and "It looks like a good-ole system."

What The Record Shows

Jones was publicly criticizing Fincher's department for stonewalling an accountability matter at the exact same time his own undisclosed benefits from Ferrell-Benavides were active and undisclosed. By August 2025, Jones had been removed from the case's center of gravity; Fincher had replaced Ferrell-Benavides entirely. The "good-ole system" language Jones used against his own department in 2024 is now, in 2026, language others use to describe the handling of his own case.

Era Two
The Legal Fight
2026

Full detail on the prosecutor chain, suspension, and dismissal is in Chapters 7 and 8. This section covers Jones's attorneys, his attorney's broader reputation, and the Sands Anderson origin dispute specifically as it concerns him.

Attorneys

Counsel of Record — CL26-011
Mark J. KrudysThe Krudys Law Firm PLC, Richmond
Perry Henry HarroldLaw Office of Perry Henry Harrold, Martinsville

Krudys is a Richmond civil-rights attorney with a long, well-documented practice of suing law enforcement agencies and jails statewide for large sums — including a $77 million suit against Stafford County over a fatal deputy shooting, a Norfolk jail death case, and a $20 million Newport News jail suicide suit. Star News TV has publicly framed his involvement in Jones's case under the headline "LAWYER MARK KRUDYS HOPES TO CASH IN ON LOCAL TAXPAYERS," grouping Jones's suit together with Ferrell-Benavides's suit and an unrelated $25 million Henry County case (Timothy Howell, not a Krudys client) under one taxpayer-cost narrative.

Open Question

Howell's case is legally and factually separate from Jones's and Ferrell-Benavides's — the only connection is Krudys's broader practice and Star News's editorial choice to bundle the stories. Worth watching whether future coverage continues to conflate them.

The Sands Anderson Origin Dispute

Contested Claim — Both Sides Shown

Jones's claim (federal lawsuit, 2026): the entire investigation was retaliatory. He and Ferrell-Benavides allege it began only after they questioned Sands Anderson's legal fees, which had increased 245% in a single fiscal year. The suit alleges Sands Anderson "manufactured" the bribery/corruption claims and fed that "work product" to the press, the Commonwealth's Attorney, and the court.

Rawls's direct rebuttal, on the record: "Sands Anderson manufactured nothing. They received disclosures from employees, including the chief financial officer of the city who observed this wrongdoing." This points back to Stanfield's whistleblower letter — documented in Chapter 1 — as the actual origin.

Both are sworn statements made in active federal litigation. Neither has been adjudicated as of this writing.

Investigator Interference

What The Record Shows

Investigators on both the workplace investigation and the Brown Edwards forensic audit cited Jones's interference as "a detriment to their work" — a documented characterization from the investigators themselves, separate from any claim made by Jones or against him in the lawsuits.

Era Three
Aftermath / Reelection
2026 – Present

CL26-011 was dismissed with prejudice May 15, 2026, with $40,000 in attorneys' fees awarded. Full timeline in Chapter 8. This section covers Jones's own public conduct and statements since.

Public Statements and Social Media

Jones is active on Facebook, posting publicly on case coverage. On the day CL26-011 was dismissed, he posted a quote attributed to his grandmother alongside criticism of local journalism. He watches and reacts to Star News TV's coverage of his own case, documented in screenshots.

The Reelection Announcement

March 21, 2026 — while still suspended and awaiting jury trial — Jones announced he will seek reelection. Full detail in Chapter 8 and the dossier index.

Community Voice — Jennifer Bowles

Bowles, a former Vice Mayor active in Facebook comments on Star News TV coverage, has repeatedly defended the broader narrative that favors Jones and Ferrell-Benavides's accounts, while labeling other community commenters "fake page" accounts and "conspiracy theorists" for raising accountability concerns. She has separately pushed a city/county service-consolidation proposal, framing it as practical cost-sharing while critics call it disguised reversion planning. She referenced Jones and Rawls together as "anti-reversion" candidates in one comment — a notable on-record statement linking the two as aligned on at least one issue, despite being on opposite sides of the broader case.

Open Question

Bowles has referenced a person called "Todd" as central to an alleged scheme behind Jones's removal proceedings, in content titled "A Scheme Set Up By Todd." The original post is no longer publicly accessible in its original form, confirmed across multiple unconnected viewing accounts. Todd's identity remains unconfirmed — possibly Todd King, a separate commenter who debated tax policy with Bowles in an unrelated thread, but this is not established.
