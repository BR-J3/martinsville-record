Person Record · Full Account

Peyton Nibblett

Assistant to the City Manager & Deputy City Clerk, City of Martinsville — June 2024 to Present

He holds two jobs that, together, touch nearly everything in this record: one gives him direct visibility into the city manager's office during the exact period it was under investigation, the other gives him custody of council's own meeting records, correspondence, and official documents. He is a subpoenaed witness in CL26-011 — not for what he did, but for what he would have seen.

Era One
Joining The City
June 2024

Nibblett joined the City of Martinsville in June 2024 — already inside the window this record covers, roughly two months after the Las Vegas trip documented in Chapter 2 and well before any of the conflict that followed became public. He holds a degree in Learning and Organizational Change and Psychology from Northwestern University, and is a commissioned Virginia Notary Public.

His dual title is the notable thing here. As Assistant to the City Manager, he worked directly inside Aretha Ferrell-Benavides's office during the entire period documented in Chapters 1 through 4 — the closed-session raise, the two leaves, the firing. As Deputy City Clerk, he separately holds custody of council's meeting records, official correspondence, and documents — the institutional memory of the council itself. His job description also covers operational studies, strategic planning, media relations, and the city's social media and website management.

Open Question

Earlier research material in this hub spells his name two ways — "Nibblett" (matching the official CL26-011 subpoena list and the July 2025 Facebook-post reporting) and "Niblett" (in earlier, less-verified notes). This page uses "Nibblett" as the more authoritative spelling, but the discrepancy itself is logged here rather than silently resolved.

What The Record Shows

Two separate roles, each with a different kind of access, sitting in one person: direct visibility into the city manager's day-to-day operations, and formal custody of the council's own records. That combination is almost certainly why he appears on the subpoena list in Era Three below — not because of any allegation against him, but because of what he would have necessarily seen and filed in the ordinary course of both jobs.

Era Two
The Archived Post
July 5, 2025

A City of Martinsville Facebook post announcing a rescheduling of the July 8, 2025 council meeting was taken down shortly after public comments — including from Councilman Julian Mei — were posted in the thread. Mei said he was never notified the original post was going out, and that when he went to check tagged-comment alerts, the post itself was gone. Full context: the broader Mei transparency complaint, documented separately in this research hub.

NIBBLETT-POST The City's Explanation, Via Nibblett Jul 5, 2025
  • The city's explanation, attributed to Deputy City Clerk Peyton Nibblett: the post was not deleted but "archived," because council had not yet voted on the proposed meeting date change
  • A vote was instead scheduled for a special meeting July 7, 2025
  • This happened eight days before Ferrell-Benavides's second administrative leave (July 23, 2025) and the formal authorization of the Brown Edwards forensic audit
  • Separately and significantly: the city's Public Information Officer position (formerly held by Kendall Davis) had just been eliminated in the new fiscal year budget, effective July 1, 2025 — as of this story, no one had been publicly identified as currently managing the city's Facebook account
Source: News story dated July 5, 2025, confirmed in hand; publication not specified in material reviewed
What The Record Shows

"Archived" is the city's own word for what happened to a public post with constituent and council comments attached, given to the press by the one person whose job actually covers the city's social media management. No claim is made here that Nibblett himself decided to take the post down, or that "archived" was a misleading characterization rather than an accurate procedural one — only that he is the named source for the explanation, at a moment when the city had no separately identified communications staff at all.

Open Question

Was the decision to archive the post Nibblett's own, a directive from the city manager's office, or a standard procedure applied automatically whenever a meeting-date item hasn't yet been voted on? Nothing in the source reviewed distinguishes between those three possibilities.
