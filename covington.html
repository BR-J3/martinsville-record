Patti Covington

Petition Organizer, CL26-011 — December 2025–Present

A resident who voted for LC Jones in 2022 and lost confidence in him gathered nearly 500 signatures by hand in three weeks, signed her name to a federal-style cover letter to a sitting circuit court judge, and watched the case dismissed on a signature count she'd been told by a different official source was already met. None of that makes her wrong about what she was trying to do — it makes the record worth getting precisely right on her behalf.

Era One
The Petition Drive
December 2025 – January 2026

Covington organized the citizen petition to remove Mayor LC Jones from office, beginning quietly in mid-December 2025. She is named in multiple Star News TV reports as the effort's principal administrator, and is confirmed in the petition's own circulator affidavit as Martha Patrice Covington — the legal circulator of record.

Mary Ann McConnell, a retired nurse, assisted with the drive. Covington personally communicated with the Virginia Department of Elections during the collection process and personally drafted and signed the transmittal letter accompanying the petition's filing with Circuit Court Judge G. Carter Greer on January 13, 2026.

"I voted for L.C. Jones in the 2022 election, but have since lost confidence."
— Patti Covington, cover letter to Judge Greer, January 13, 2026

The letter cited both the Sands Anderson investigation and the Brown Edwards forensic audit as legal review of information "paid for by but concealed from taxpayers," stated there were fact witnesses among current and former city employees willing to testify, and referenced an attached email from Paul Goldman attempting to dissuade citizens from signing. It was copied to Commonwealth's Attorney Patrick Flinn.

The Number She Was Given

Martinsville's registrar, Sarah Workman, had publicly stated the threshold was 375 signatures — a figure later confirmed to trace to the 2022 election cycle. The court ultimately applied a different number: 682, based on total votes cast across the multi-candidate November 2022 council race in which Jones won his seat. The petition's 401 certified valid signatures cleared Workman's 375 with room to spare, but fell 281 short of the court's 682.

What The Record Shows

Covington was told by the city's own registrar that she needed 375 signatures, and gathered well past that number. The court instead applied a higher figure rooted in a different reading of which election year and which vote count governs Jones's specific seat — a genuine legal dispute among three different officials (Workman's 375, former CA Andy Hall's 571, and the court's 682), not a case of an organizer falling short of a goal she'd actually been given. Full detail on the threshold dispute itself is in Chapter 6.

Era Two
The Dismissal
May 15, 2026

CL26-011 was dismissed with prejudice on May 15, 2026, with $40,000 in attorneys' fees awarded — full detail in Chapter 8. Jones's attorney, Mark Krudys, argued in open court that the petition was "meritless," that denying fees would tell future officials "the defense of a meritless removal petition is theirs to bear and to bear alone," and separately named Aaron Rawls and Eric Payne as the two people who had "an axe to grind" in connection with the case.

Open Question

The dismissal turned entirely on the signature-count question above — the court never reached the underlying conflict-of-interest and bribery allegations the petition raised. "Meritless" in this context describes the petition's procedural outcome, not a judicial finding that its underlying concerns lacked substance. Whether those underlying concerns hold up is a separate question this record does not consider settled by the dismissal itself.

Era Three
Accountability Advocate
2026 – Present

Covington has remained an active, precise public commenter since the dismissal — consistently distinguishing procedural outcomes from substantive ones, and pushing back directly when she sees that distinction collapsed.

"The registrar verified and certified that it met the 375 minimum she communicated to the circuit court and the commonwealth attorney determined that it had merit and officially filed the petition with the circuit court. That responsibility falls under their respective positions, it is not the responsibility of citizens... The continued deflection by some to Aaron or the cost of investigations & court proceeding ignores the elephant in the room — THE BEHAVIOR, including the continued concealment of the [workplace investigation]."
— Patti Covington, Facebook, addressing Melody Cartwright's process critique

On April 21, 2026, with both the Brown Edwards audit and the Sands Anderson workplace investigation reportedly complete but not yet public, Covington was the source who confirmed the VSP investigation remained active: "The Virginia State Police are still investigating, and I don't think anything can interfere with that."

"Having lived in the former Soviet Union (Moscow) almost 2 years in the mid 90s, I assure you it ain't pretty when citizens have no voice. Martinsville has intelligent and invested citizens of all colors and ethnic backgrounds who stood up for transparency and accountability in our government."
— Patti Covington, Facebook, Memorial Day 2026

Not a Simple Faction

Covington's record doesn't sort neatly into a single camp. In November 2025 — before the petition existed — she defended council's unanimous, Rawls-supported approval of a 250th Anniversary race-event donation against Melody Cartwright's "good ole boy" framing of the same vote. The two women were not aligned then, and their alignment since has shifted by issue rather than settled into a fixed pattern.

What The Record Shows

Across every documented statement, Covington consistently separates who is legally responsible for a given decision from who is politically convenient to blame for it — applying that same discipline whether the question is the petition's signature math, the registrar's role versus the Commonwealth's Attorney's, or a council vote she had no part in organizing. That consistency is itself part of the record, not just her conclusions.
