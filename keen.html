Reva Keen

Henry County Deputy Sheriff — Assigned to City Council Security

Keen has not made any public statement about the events on this page. Nearly everything documented about her role comes from one party's account in an active federal lawsuit, not from her own words or an independent finding. This page is written with that limitation deliberately kept visible throughout, rather than treating one side's complaint as settled fact.

Era One
The Ejection
March 25, 2025

Keen is a Henry County Deputy Sheriff, not a Martinsville city police officer — assigned to provide security at Martinsville City Council meetings. On March 25, 2025, during a council meeting, she physically removed Council Member Aaron Rawls from chambers. What follows is drawn almost entirely from Rawls's own federal complaint, filed two months later — it is his account, not an adjudicated record of what happened.

RAWLS-COMPLAINT Rawls v. Keen & Ferrell-Benavides — Allegations Regarding Keen's Conduct As alleged, filed Jun 2, 2025
  • Per the complaint: by the time Keen reached Rawls's chair, his exchange with Mayor Jones had already ended, and another council member had begun speaking
  • Per the complaint: Rawls was not attempting to continue speaking or interrupt anyone when Keen approached
  • Per the complaint: Keen physically seized Rawls, required him to leave his chair, and told him, "Outside. Go on outside."
  • Per the complaint: at no point did Mayor Jones or anyone else verbally direct Keen to remove Rawls
  • Per the complaint: Keen acted on a non-verbal signal from City Manager Ferrell-Benavides, visible on the meeting recording
  • The complaint characterizes this as a "pre-conceived plan" between Keen and Ferrell-Benavides — this is the plaintiff's characterization, not a court finding
Source: Rawls federal complaint, Case 4:25-cv-00028, confirmed in hand. Allegations only.
Open Question

Keen has not filed a public response describing the incident in her own words, and no source reviewed includes any statement from her about her own reasoning, instructions she believed she had received, or her understanding of her authority in that moment. Whether she was in fact directed by Ferrell-Benavides, acted on her own judgment, or understood the situation differently than the complaint describes is not established by anything other than the opposing party's filing.

The complaint also notes that minutes after Rawls was removed, Ferrell-Benavides spoke to the remaining council, praising her own performance and stating she was underpaid relative to peer city managers — a moment Rawls was not present for, since he had already been escorted out.

Era Two
The Federal Defendant
June 2025 – Present

Rawls filed suit against Keen and Ferrell-Benavides, both in their personal capacities, on June 2, 2025, in the U.S. District Court for the Western District of Virginia, Danville Division — Case 4:25-cv-00028. The complaint brings four counts under 42 U.S.C. §1983: First Amendment viewpoint discrimination, Fourth Amendment unreasonable seizure, deprivation of liberty, and Fourteenth Amendment equal protection.

The Signal — Disputed

Rawls's position: Ferrell-Benavides gave Keen a non-verbal signal, visible on video, directing the removal — part of a coordinated plan between the two of them.

Ferrell-Benavides's position (response filed July 2025): Her legal team has formally denied that any such signal was given.

As of the most recent filing reviewed, Keen had not yet filed her own response to the complaint. Her position on what happened — whether she received any signal, verbal or otherwise, and from whom — is not independently on the record.

Keen has also been subpoenaed to testify in the now-dismissed CL26-011 removal proceeding, in connection with the broader events of that period.

What The Record Shows

Keen is a named defendant in active federal litigation over a specific, video-recorded incident — that much is fact. What remains genuinely unresolved is intent and instruction: whether she was directed, by whom, and what she understood her own authority to be in that moment. Absent her own account or a court ruling, this page documents the allegation clearly as an allegation, not as settled history.
