The Principal Actors

LC Jones Mayor — City of Martinsville

Elected mayor. Signed Ferrell-Benavides's 15% pay raise alone, in closed session, with no legal counsel present. Sole dissenting vote on her July 21, 2025 raise, her July 23, 2025 administrative leave, and her August 7, 2025 termination. Received airfare, a hot tub, employment assistance, and legal arrangement — none disclosed on Statement of Economic Interests. Suspended February 2026. Reinstated April 21, 2026. CL26-011 dismissed with prejudice May 15, 2026. Filed federal lawsuit against Rawls and Sands Anderson June 9, 2026. On March 21, 2026, while still suspended and awaiting jury trial, Jones announced he will seek reelection to the City Council in the November 2026 election.

Status: Mayor — seeking reelection November 2026
Aretha Ferrell-Benavides City Manager — October 2023 to August 2025

Hired October 2023. Previously city manager of Duncanville, TX and Petersburg, VA. Nichols Jackson forensic audit of her Duncanville tenure released March 20, 2024 — while she was serving in Martinsville. Provided Jones with undisclosed personal benefits. City purchasing card records from January 2024 through March 2025 document recurring multi-night Washington, DC hotel stays and travel charges totaling tens of thousands of dollars across the disclosure period. Took an unexplained leave May 21–June 10, 2025, invoking federal protection and her personal attorney when pressed for the reason. Received a raise from $183,500 to $215,000 on July 21, 2025. Placed on a second, paid administrative leave July 23, 2025, tied to credit card controversy and the authorization of the Brown Edwards forensic audit. Fired August 7, 2025, 4-1 vote; status briefly reverted to suspended on August 12 for city charter compliance before termination took effect again. Filed EEOC discrimination complaint before termination. Has stated publicly that racial tension played a factor in her dismissal — a claim neither confirmed nor disproven in the public record. Filed federal lawsuit against City of Martinsville, Rawls, and Sands Anderson June 9, 2026 — seeking $220,000+ unpaid severance.

Status: Former city manager — federal plaintiff as of June 9, 2026
Aaron Rawls City Council Member — Martinsville

Council member. Publicly opposed Ferrell-Benavides pay raise March 25, 2025 — not knowing it had already been signed. Removed from council chambers by Deputy Sheriff Keen on nonverbal signal. Learned of signed raise via news media six weeks later. Filed federal complaint 4:25-cv-00028 June 2, 2025. Voted to terminate Ferrell-Benavides August 7, 2025. Subpoenaed to testify in CL26-011. Named defendant in Jones v. Rawls and Sands Anderson (June 9, 2026) and Ferrell-Benavides v. City et al. (June 9, 2026). Not seeking re-election November 2026.

Status: Council member — term expires November 2026 · Federal defendant as of June 9, 2026
J. Richard Stanfield Chief Financial Officer — City of Martinsville

City CFO. Sent internal memo to Ferrell-Benavides and Reese-Atmore March 13, 2025 — no resolution. Sent formal whistleblower letter to Sands Anderson April 13, 2025 — initiated the investigation that produced everything that followed. Warned city leadership about missed US Treasury ARPA filing deadlines. Subpoenaed to testify in CL26-011.

Status: City CFO — active as of publication
Robert Fincher Police Chief; City Manager — August 2025 to present

31 years with the Martinsville Police Department, including three years as Chief of Police. Named point of contact for city manager business during Ferrell-Benavides's first leave (May–June 2025). Appointed interim/acting city manager following her second leave (July 2025) and again after her termination. Discovered March 18 offer letter — Jones's sole signature — in Ferrell-Benavides's city email draft folder. Discovery documented in Sands Anderson investigation. Subpoenaed to testify in CL26-011. Named defendant in Johnson v. Fincher and Rawls. Formally appointed permanent City Manager by council vote, March 2026 — no longer interim.

Status: City Manager (permanent, as of March 2026) · Federal defendant
Reva Keen Deputy Sheriff — Martinsville

Deputy Sheriff assigned to city council security. Removed Aaron Rawls from council chambers March 25, 2025 on nonverbal signal from Ferrell-Benavides — without verbal order from presiding officer, without citing a specific rule violation. Named defendant in Rawls federal complaint 4:25-cv-00028. Subpoenaed to testify in CL26-011.

Status: Named federal defendant — 4:25-cv-00028 active
Julian Mei City Council Member — Martinsville

Council member. Voted to terminate Ferrell-Benavides August 7, 2025 — part of 4-1 majority. Publicly stated on citizen petition: citizens have the right to use the lawful process to remove officials from office. Said he last saw Ferrell-Benavides about a month before her termination. Subpoenaed to testify in CL26-011. Not seeking re-election in 2026 — term expires 2028 alongside Kathy Lawson and Rayshaun Gravely. Confirmed directly by Patti Covington, June 2026.

Status: Council member — term expires 2028
Rayshaun O'Shea Gravely City Council Member — Martinsville

Council member. Voted to terminate Ferrell-Benavides August 7, 2025 — part of 4-1 majority. Stated publicly that the scope of the underlying investigation extended beyond the city manager alone, a claim made around the same time Ferrell-Benavides separately attributed her dismissal in part to racial tension. Term expires 2028 — not on November 2026 ballot. Separately facing charges in Martinsville GDC for driving on a suspended license and an expired vehicle inspection, unrelated to the governance matters in this record; court date set for July 23, 2026.

Status: Council member — term expires 2028 · Separate GDC court date July 23, 2026
Andy Hall Commonwealth's Attorney — Martinsville (2017–2026)

Commonwealth's Attorney since December 2017, succeeding Clay Gravely. Cited 571-signature threshold on citizen petition — higher than Workman's 375, lower than Brinks's court-ruled 1,179. Role in early CL26-011 proceedings. Named defendant in Mook v. Hall federal case. Announced January 2026 he will not seek reelection after two terms, endorsing his Deputy Commonwealth's Attorney, Patrick Flinn, as successor.

Status: Commonwealth's Attorney, not seeking reelection 2026 · Named federal defendant
Patrick Flinn Commonwealth's Attorney — Filed CL26-011

Filed the formal removal petition CL26-011 against Jones. Noted that Jones and Ferrell-Benavides refused to cooperate with the investigation. Proceeded on willing witnesses only. Case dismissed with prejudice May 15, 2026 under third special prosecutor Alfred Gray Collins III. Formerly Deputy Commonwealth's Attorney under Andy Hall; endorsed by Hall as successor ahead of the 2026 election.

Status: Commonwealth's Attorney — CL26-011 dismissed
Alfred Gray Collins III Third Special Prosecutor — CL26-011

Appointed April 28, 2026 as third special prosecutor in CL26-011 — seventeen days before dismissal. Prosecutor of record when case was dismissed with prejudice May 15, 2026.

Status: Case dismissed — no further proceedings on CL26-011
Eric Payne Witness — Hot Tub and Legal Arrangement

Eyewitness to hot tub at Ferrell-Benavides residence September 2024. Observed delivered hot tub at Jones residence December 12, 2024. Summoned by Ferrell-Benavides to advise Jones on HCSD employment matter Spring 2025. Subpoenaed to testify in CL26-011.

Status: Named witness — subpoenaed in CL26-011
Karen Roberts Executive Assistant — City Manager / Clerk of Council

Sent email to Jones June 13, 2024 requesting reimbursement for Las Vegas airfare charged to city credit card. Contemporary record of city's awareness that personal travel had been charged to government account. Subpoenaed to testify in CL26-011.

Status: Named witness — subpoenaed in CL26-011
Patti Covington Citizen Petition Organizer

Principal administrator of citizen petition drive to remove Jones. Organized signature collection December 2025 through February 2026. Petition collected 401 certified valid signatures — fell short of Judge Brinks's 1,179 threshold by 778. Remained a documented public source on the underlying investigation after the petition's outcome — publicly confirmed Virginia State Police investigation active as of April 21, 2026, the same day Jones was reinstated to office.

Status: Private citizen — VSP confirmation on record April 21, 2026

The November 2026 Election

Julian Mei, Kathy Lawson, and Rayshaun Gravely hold seats with terms expiring in 2028 and are not on the November 2026 ballot.

What The Record Shows

Community members who spoke publicly about accountability in Martinsville's city government were labeled fake, called conspiracy theorists, had their comments deleted, and received threatening private messages. The documented record of that pressure is part of this record — as much as the court filings and the offer letter.
