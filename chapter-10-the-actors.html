The Principal Actors

LC Jones Mayor — City of Martinsville

Elected mayor. Signed Ferrell-Benavides's 15% pay raise alone, in closed session, with no legal counsel present. Sole dissenting vote on her termination. Received airfare, a hot tub, employment assistance, and legal arrangement — none disclosed on Statement of Economic Interests. Suspended February 2026. Reinstated April 21, 2026. CL26-011 dismissed with prejudice May 15, 2026. Filed federal lawsuit against Rawls and Sands Anderson June 9, 2026. Not seeking re-election November 2026.

Status: Mayor — active term through November 2026 election
Aretha Ferrell-Benavides City Manager — October 2023 to August 2025

Hired October 2023. Previously city manager of Duncanville, TX and Petersburg, VA. Nichols Jackson forensic audit of her Duncanville tenure released March 20, 2024 — while she was serving in Martinsville. Provided Jones with undisclosed personal benefits. Placed on administrative leave July 2025 — publicly described as personal leave. Filed EEOC discrimination complaint before termination. Fired August 2025, 4-1 vote. Filed federal lawsuit against City of Martinsville, Rawls, and Sands Anderson June 9, 2026 — seeking $220,000+ unpaid severance.

Status: Former city manager — federal plaintiff as of June 9, 2026
Aaron Rawls City Council Member — Martinsville

Council member. Publicly opposed Ferrell-Benavides pay raise March 25, 2025 — not knowing it had already been signed. Removed from council chambers by Deputy Sheriff Keen on nonverbal signal. Learned of signed raise via news media six weeks later. Filed federal complaint 4:25-cv-00028 June 2, 2025. Subpoenaed to testify in CL26-011. Named defendant in Jones v. Rawls and Sands Anderson (June 9, 2026) and Ferrell-Benavides v. City et al. (June 9, 2026). Not seeking re-election November 2026.

Status: Council member — active term · Federal defendant as of June 9, 2026
J. Richard Stanfield Chief Financial Officer — City of Martinsville

City CFO. Sent internal memo to Ferrell-Benavides and Reese-Atmore March 13, 2025 — no resolution. Sent formal whistleblower letter to Sands Anderson April 13, 2025 — initiated the investigation that produced everything that followed. Warned city leadership about missed US Treasury ARPA filing deadlines. Subpoenaed to testify in CL26-011.

Status: City CFO — active as of publication
Robert Fincher Interim City Manager — August 2025 to present

Appointed interim city manager after Ferrell-Benavides termination. Discovered March 18 offer letter — Jones's sole signature — in Ferrell-Benavides's city email draft folder. Discovery documented in Sands Anderson investigation. Subpoenaed to testify in CL26-011. Named defendant in Johnson v. Fincher and Rawls.

Status: Interim City Manager — active · Federal defendant
Reva Keen Deputy Sheriff — Martinsville

Deputy Sheriff assigned to city council security. Removed Aaron Rawls from council chambers March 25, 2025 on nonverbal signal from Ferrell-Benavides — without verbal order from presiding officer, without citing a specific rule violation. Named defendant in Rawls federal complaint 4:25-cv-00028. Subpoenaed to testify in CL26-011.

Status: Named federal defendant — 4:25-cv-00028 active
Julian Mei City Council Member — Martinsville

Council member. Voted to terminate Ferrell-Benavides — part of 4-1 majority. Publicly stated on citizen petition: citizens have the right to use the lawful process to remove officials from office. Subpoenaed to testify in CL26-011. Seeking re-election November 2026.

Status: Council member — seeking re-election November 2026
Andy Hall Commonwealth's Attorney — Martinsville

Commonwealth's Attorney. Cited 571-signature threshold on citizen petition — higher than Workman's 375, lower than Brinks's court-ruled 682. Role in early CL26-011 proceedings. Named defendant in Mook v. Hall federal case.

Status: Commonwealth's Attorney · Named federal defendant
Patrick Flinn Commonwealth's Attorney — Filed CL26-011

Filed the formal removal petition CL26-011 against Jones. Noted that Jones and Ferrell-Benavides refused to cooperate with the investigation. Proceeded on willing witnesses only. Case dismissed with prejudice May 15, 2026 under third special prosecutor Alfred Gray Collins III.

Status: Commonwealth's Attorney — CL26-011 dismissed
Alfred Gray Collins III Third Special Prosecutor — CL26-011

Appointed April 28, 2026 as third special prosecutor in CL26-011 — seventeen days before dismissal. Prosecutor of record when case was dismissed with prejudice May 15, 2026.

Status: Case dismissed — no further proceedings on CL26-011
Eric Payne Witness — Hot Tub and Legal Arrangement

Eyewitness to hot tub at Ferrell-Benavides residence September 2024. Observed delivered hot tub at Jones residence December 12, 2024. Summoned by Ferrell-Benavides to advise Jones on HCSD employment matter Spring 2025. Subpoenaed to testify in CL26-011.

Status: Named witness — subpoenaed in CL26-011
Karen Roberts Executive Assistant — City Manager / Clerk of Council

Sent email to Jones June 13, 2024 requesting reimbursement for Las Vegas airfare charged to city credit card. Contemporary record of city's awareness that personal travel had been charged to government account. Subpoenaed to testify in CL26-011.

Status: Named witness — subpoenaed in CL26-011
Patti Covington Citizen Petition Organizer

Principal administrator of citizen petition drive to remove Jones. Organized signature collection December 2025 through February 2026. Petition collected 375+ valid signatures — fell short of Judge Brinks's 682 threshold. Publicly confirmed Virginia State Police investigation active as of April 21, 2026.

Status: Private citizen — VSP confirmation on record April 21, 2026

What The Record Shows

Community members who spoke publicly about accountability in Martinsville's city government were labeled fake, called conspiracy theorists, had their comments deleted, and received threatening private messages. The documented record of that pressure is part of this record — as much as the court filings and the offer letter.
