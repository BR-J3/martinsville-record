Chapter Four

The Firing

Two Leaves · A Raise · 4-1 Vote · Sands Anderson

Martinsville's city manager took two separate leaves in the summer of 2025 — the first unexplained, the second tied directly to a credit card controversy and a $20,000 forensic audit. In between, the council gave her a raise. She was fired 4-1 two weeks later.

The Record

By the summer of 2025, the Sands Anderson investigation initiated by Stanfield's April 13 whistleblower letter was active. Over the following three months, Martinsville's city manager took two separate leaves of absence, received a raise, and was ultimately terminated — in that order. The two leaves are distinct events, separated by five weeks, and they should not be confused with each other.

The First Leave — May 21 to June 10, 2025

On May 21, 2025, Star News TV reported that sources told the outlet Ferrell-Benavides would be taking leave for "at least 2 weeks to handle some personal issues," with Police Chief Robert Fincher named as point of contact for city manager business during her absence. No city statement at the time named a cause.

The next day, after what Star News TV described as multiple requests for comment, Ferrell-Benavides responded directly by email. Her response did not explain the reason for the leave. It explained why she would not.

"Thank you for your inquiry. I would like to clarify that I am not on vacation, but rather on personal leave protected under federal law. I will be away for the next two weeks, and the conditions surrounding this leave are confidential and legally protected. Due to the nature of your request and the potential for accessing or disclosing protected information regarding the specifics of my leave, I have included my personal attorney in this correspondence. Thank you for your understanding."
— Aretha R. Ferrell-Benavides, email to Star News TV, May 22, 2025

What The Record Shows

Ferrell-Benavides took an unexplained two-and-a-half-week leave in late May and early June 2025, before the credit card controversy that triggered her second leave had become public. When pressed for the reason, she responded by invoking federal legal protection for the confidentiality of the leave itself and brought her personal attorney into a routine press inquiry. The underlying reason for this first leave has never been publicly disclosed.

The Raise — July 21, 2025

One day before her second leave began, the Martinsville City Council formally approved a raise for Ferrell-Benavides — from $183,500 to $215,000 — following weeks of negotiation. The vote was 3-2. Mayor LC Jones was the lone dissenting vote.

  • Council vote: 3-2 to approve raise
  • Salary change: $183,500 to $215,000
  • Followed weeks of negotiation

Source: Cardinal News, July 25, 2025, confirmed in hand
What The Record Shows

The council raised Ferrell-Benavides's salary by more than $31,000 on July 21. The next day it placed her on paid administrative leave. The day after that, it authorized a forensic audit of her spending. Jones dissented from both the raise and the leave vote — the same pattern of sole dissent documented throughout this record.

The Second Leave — July 23, 2025

On July 23, 2025, the council voted 4-1 to place Ferrell-Benavides on paid administrative leave following a specially called closed session. The same evening, council voted to allocate $20,000 to the Brown Edwards accounting firm for a forensic audit of the city's financial books. Mayor Jones again cast the sole dissenting vote on both measures.

The leave followed public controversy over city credit card statements — including near-monthly trips, meals, and a nearly $2,000 golf outing for city employees — first surfaced by WDBJ7 in May 2025 from records obtained via Aaron Rawls's FOIA request. On the same day the council placed her on leave, a Texas city announced Ferrell-Benavides as a finalist for its own city manager position.

The EEOC Complaint and the Racial-Tension Claim

Ferrell-Benavides filed a complaint with the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission alleging discrimination before her termination vote. Separately, in a public statement reported by the Martinsville Bulletin, she said racial tension played a factor in her dismissal. Councilman Rayshaun Gravely, addressing the same period, said the scope of the underlying investigation extended beyond the city manager alone.

CH4-EEOC EEOC Complaint and Public Statements Jul–Aug 2025
  • EEOC discrimination complaint filed by Ferrell-Benavides — prior to termination vote
  • Ferrell-Benavides public statement: racial tension was a factor in her dismissal
  • Rayshaun Gravely public statement: investigation scope extended beyond the city manager
  • Both statements on the record, reported separately from the EEOC filing itself
Source: Martinsville Bulletin, July 26, 2025, confirmed in hand · Martinsville Research Hub
What The Record Shows

Ferrell-Benavides has publicly attributed her dismissal in part to racial tension. This record does not adjudicate that claim — it documents that she made it, on the record, and that a sitting council member separately suggested the investigation's scope was not limited to her alone. Neither statement has been independently confirmed or disproven in the public record as of this writing.

The Vote — August 7, 2025, 4 to 1

The council cited four grounds for termination: commission of an act involving malfeasance and dishonesty, violation of the city charter, violation of city code/policy/rule or regulation, and unsatisfactory performance. After the vote, council also directed that pertinent facts of the Sands Anderson investigation be provided to the Commonwealth's Attorney.

What The Record Shows

Mayor LC Jones voted against the termination of the city manager whose raise he had voted against three weeks earlier, and whose administrative leave he had also voted against. He was the only vote against termination. The same pattern — Jones as sole dissent on every action related to Ferrell-Benavides or the investigation — repeats throughout the record.

The Charter Compliance Reversal — August 12, 2025

On August 12, 2025, the council voted 3-2 to temporarily amend its termination decision to comply with notice procedures required under Chapter 5, Section 1 of the city charter. Ferrell-Benavides's status switched from "fired" to "suspended" while the city attorney provided her written notice detailing the specific reasons for removal, as the charter required. Her formal termination took effect again following that process.

CH4-CHARTER Charter Compliance Reversal Aug 12, 2025
  • Aug 12, 2025 — council votes 3-2 to amend prior termination decision for charter compliance
  • Status temporarily reverts from "fired" to "suspended with pay"
  • City attorney directed to provide written notice of specific termination grounds, per Chapter 5, Section 1 of the city charter
  • Termination subsequently takes effect again following charter-compliant notice
Source: Henry County Enterprise, August 15, 2025, confirmed in hand · Cardinal News, August 13, 2025, confirmed in hand

Robert Fincher — Appointed Interim City Manager

Following Ferrell-Benavides's termination, the council appointed Robert Fincher as Interim City Manager. It was Fincher who subsequently discovered the March 18 offer letter — bearing Jones's sole signature — in her city email draft folder. That discovery became a key piece of documentation in the Sands Anderson investigation.

CH4-FINCHER Fincher — Interim Appointment and Offer Letter Discovery Aug 2025
  • Fincher appointed Interim City Manager following Ferrell-Benavides termination
  • Discovered March 18 offer letter in Ferrell-Benavides city email draft folder
  • Offer letter had not been formally provided to the council or city records
  • Fincher subpoenaed to testify in CL26-011
  • Named as defendant in Johnson v. Fincher and Rawls federal suit
Source: Martinsville Research Hub · CL26-011 subpoena list · Rawls Affidavit Jan 12, 2026

The Severance Dispute

Ferrell-Benavides's termination triggered a dispute over severance. By June 2026, she had filed a federal lawsuit seeking $220,000 or more in unpaid severance, in addition to other claims.

Taxpayer Exposure — Active

The Ferrell-Benavides federal suit filed June 9, 2026 seeks $220,000 or more in unpaid severance plus additional damages. The City of Martinsville — meaning taxpayers — is a named defendant. Chapter 9 covers all five active federal cases and total taxpayer exposure.
