Chapter Four

The Firing

August 2025 · 4-1 Vote · Sands Anderson · Investigation Launched

In August 2025, the Martinsville City Council voted 4-1 to terminate City Manager Aretha Ferrell-Benavides. Mayor LC Jones cast the sole dissenting vote. The Sands Anderson investigation was already underway. Ferrell-Benavides had already filed EEOC discrimination charges before the vote was taken.

The Record

By the summer of 2025, the Sands Anderson investigation initiated by Stanfield's April 13 whistleblower letter was active. The city had placed Ferrell-Benavides on administrative leave. She had publicly described her absence as personal leave. In July 2025, before her termination, she filed an EEOC discrimination complaint against the city.

In August 2025, the Martinsville City Council voted to terminate her employment. The vote was 4 to 1. Mayor LC Jones cast the only vote against termination — the same mayor whose sole signature appeared on her 15% pay raise letter five months earlier.

Administrative Leave — The "Personal Leave" Statement

Before the termination vote, Ferrell-Benavides was placed on administrative leave while the Sands Anderson investigation proceeded. During this period, a public statement characterized her absence as personal leave.

The EEOC Complaint — Filed Before the Vote

In July 2025 — before the council voted to terminate her — Ferrell-Benavides filed a complaint with the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission alleging discrimination. The complaint named the City of Martinsville.

The timing is documented: the EEOC complaint was filed while she was on administrative leave and before the termination vote. Also in July 2025, Ferrell-Benavides was named as a finalist for the city manager position in Elgin, Texas — suggesting active pursuit of outside employment during the same period.

The Vote — 4 to 1

In August 2025, the Martinsville City Council convened and voted on the termination of City Manager Aretha Ferrell-Benavides. The vote was four in favor of termination, one opposed.

Council Vote — Termination of City Manager Ferrell-Benavides — August 2025
Aaron Rawls YES — Terminate
Julian Mei YES — Terminate
Tammy Pearson YES — Terminate
Danny Turner YES — Terminate
LC Jones — Mayor NO — Sole Dissent

Jones was the only vote against termination. He had signed her pay raise alone five months earlier. He had cast the sole dissenting vote — a pattern that would repeat across every consequential council action involving Ferrell-Benavides or the investigation that followed her.

What The Record Shows

Mayor LC Jones voted against the termination of the city manager whose 15% pay raise he had signed alone, in a closed session with no legal counsel, five months earlier. He was the only vote against. The same pattern — Jones as sole dissent on every action related to Ferrell-Benavides or the investigation — repeats throughout the record.

Robert Fincher — Appointed Interim City Manager

Following Ferrell-Benavides's termination, the council appointed Robert Fincher as Interim City Manager. Fincher had previously served in city administration and was familiar with Martinsville's financial operations.

It was Fincher who subsequently discovered the March 18 offer letter — the document bearing Jones's sole signature authorizing Ferrell-Benavides's 15% pay raise — in her city email draft folder. That discovery became a key piece of documentation in the Sands Anderson investigation and the subsequent removal petition.

The Severance Dispute

Ferrell-Benavides's termination triggered a dispute over severance. Her employment contract contained severance provisions. The city's position and her position on what was owed diverged. By June 2026, she had filed a federal lawsuit — Ferrell-Benavides v. City of Martinsville, Rawls, and Sands Anderson — seeking $220,000 or more in unpaid severance, in addition to other claims.

Taxpayer Exposure — Active

The Ferrell-Benavides federal suit filed June 9, 2026 seeks $220,000 or more in unpaid severance plus additional damages. The city — meaning Martinsville taxpayers — is a named defendant.
