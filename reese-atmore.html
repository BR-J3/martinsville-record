Person Record · Full Account

Edena Reese-Atmore

Chief Operating Officer, City of Martinsville — Tenure Dates Unconfirmed

The other name on CFO Stanfield's first written warning. She arrived already linked to Ferrell-Benavides's prior Texas tenure, personally suggested backdating a federal grant document months before any of this became public, and was still making changes inside the city's financial system weeks after she was formally barred from it. She has not made any public statement addressing any of this.

Era One
The Duncanville Connection
Dates Unconfirmed

Reese-Atmore is not a Martinsville hire who happened to end up working alongside Aretha Ferrell-Benavides. Per Stanfield's own April 13, 2025 whistleblower letter, the two had worked together previously in Duncanville, Texas — the same city where a 2024 forensic audit had already documented financial-management problems under Ferrell-Benavides's tenure there (full detail: Chapter 1, and Ferrell-Benavides's own person-page). Stanfield's letter states plainly that in Duncanville, "issues almost identical to the City of Martinsville happened."

Open Question

No source reviewed so far confirms Reese-Atmore's exact title or role in Duncanville, when she was hired in Martinsville, or whether she came to Martinsville at Ferrell-Benavides's direct recommendation. The Duncanville connection itself is confirmed, sourced to Stanfield's own letter; the mechanics of how she ended up in Martinsville are not.

By March 13, 2025, Reese-Atmore was serving as Chief Operating Officer when CFO J. Richard Stanfield — then barely two and a half months into his own role — sent a signed internal memo to her and Ferrell-Benavides jointly, raising seven numbered concerns: credit card controls (134 cards issued across a staff of roughly 350), purchase-order thresholds, signature-stamp overuse, inconsistent timesheet practices, a moratorium on further chart-of-accounts changes, undone ARPA quarterly filings, and an incomplete prior-year audit. Full detail: Chapter 1 and Stanfield's own page.

What The Record Shows

Reese-Atmore was one of exactly two named recipients of the first formal written warning in this entire record. The memo produced no resolution. Thirty-one days later, Stanfield escalated outside the city manager's chain entirely — to the city's own outside legal counsel.

Era Two
The Backdating Suggestion
March – April 2025

The most serious documented act tied to Reese-Atmore concerns federal ARPA funding. Stanfield discovered the city had failed to obligate the remaining $4.4 million in ARPA funds by the December 31, 2024 deadline, despite asking Reese-Atmore about the grant repeatedly from October through December 2024 and being assured it was being handled.

[On a March 10, 2025 phone call, per Stanfield's account] Reese-Atmore suggested backdating memoranda of understanding in place of purchase orders to retroactively obligate the funds.
— Paraphrased from Stanfield's April 13, 2025 whistleblower letter to Sands Anderson; see Stanfield's own page for full context
What The Record Shows

Stanfield refused. By his own account, backdating those documents would have meant falsifying records tied to federal funds. Instead, he went around Reese-Atmore and Ferrell-Benavides entirely — contacting Delegate Eric Phillips directly, who contacted Congressman Morgan Griffith's office, who contacted the U.S. Treasury. Treasury reopened the reporting window for 24 hours on April 7, 2025, and Stanfield obligated the $4.4 million within it. Had that effort failed, the city would have been required to return the full $4.4 million to the federal government. Reese-Atmore's suggestion is documented here as Stanfield's own account of a private phone call — it has not been independently corroborated by any other source reviewed, and she has not been asked to respond to it in any source found.

The same letter documents a separate incident from this period: a consulting firm's workpapers tied to the prior year's incomplete audit had been uploaded without review. Stanfield records Reese-Atmore responding, laughing, that auditors would catch any errors in those workpapers "because she hadn't looked at them."

Open Question

Both the backdating suggestion and the workpapers comment come from Stanfield's own letter — a document this record treats as credible given how much of its other content (the ARPA deadline, the audit delays, the credit card findings) was later independently confirmed by the Brown Edwards audit. But neither specific claim about Reese-Atmore personally has a second, independent source attached yet. They are documented as Stanfield's sworn-adjacent account, not as adjudicated fact.

Era Three
Barred But Still Active
May – July 2025

By July 2025, Ferrell-Benavides had formally notified staff that Reese-Atmore was no longer to be involved in finance, budget, or procurement, effective May 20, 2025. On July 10 — roughly seven weeks after that notice — Stanfield discovered Reese-Atmore was still actively adding funds and making changes inside the city's Munis financial system.

Open Question

The gap between the May 20 effective date and the July 10 discovery is roughly seven weeks, not a day or two — worth flagging plainly rather than smoothing over. Whether her actual system access was supposed to end on May 20 specifically, or whether some other access-termination date applies that this record hasn't located, isn't resolved by any source reviewed.

Open Question

Ferrell-Benavides's 10:15 AM reply claims Reese-Atmore had moved to "a consulting firm" still completing outstanding city tasks. Whether that means she had formally left city employment by this point, was on some other contractual arrangement with the city, or something else entirely isn't established. Her actual employment status as of July 10, 2025 — let alone today — is not confirmed anywhere in this record.

The Raise, Documented Later

The April 2026 Brown Edwards hiring-practices audit, examining personnel decisions made during Ferrell-Benavides's tenure, separately found that Reese-Atmore received a $5,800/year raise with no business purpose listed on the change-of-status form. Full detail: Chapter 5. This is the same audit that documented similar undocumented raises for several other named city employees during this same period.

What The Record Shows — Overall

Reese-Atmore appears in this record entirely through other people's documents — Stanfield's memo, Stanfield's letter, an email chain between Stanfield and Ferrell-Benavides, and a later audit's personnel finding. No public statement from Reese-Atmore herself, responding to any of this, has been located. Everything on this page should be read with that asymmetry in mind: it is what was written about her and around her, not her own account.
