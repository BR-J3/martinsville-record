Person Record · Full Career

Travis Hodge

HR Generalist (2016) → HR Director (2019–2025) → Organizational Development Director (Current)

A Martinsville native who came home in 2016 to run the city's own HR department, made a public remark in a heated council meeting that the press itself questioned as a breach of his role, left the city's employ months later, and has since returned under an expanded title. This page documents the full, real arc — not just the moment that made the news.

Era One
HR Director
2016 – 2025

Hodge was born and raised in Martinsville, attending Laurel Park High School before graduating among Magna Vista High School's first consolidated class. He earned a Bachelor of Science in Business Administration from Virginia State University, an HBCU, and built a career in North Carolina specializing in employment consulting, Trade Adjustment Assistance, human resources, staffing, and recruiting, earning certifications in employment law and risk management along the way.

In 2016, he returned to his hometown to work for the City of Martinsville, starting as an HR Generalist. He was promoted to Human Resources Director in 2019, overseeing recruitment, compensation, and benefits for a department serving over 350 city employees — a tenure that placed him squarely inside city government through the entire run-up to the Ferrell-Benavides governance crisis documented elsewhere in this record.

Era Two
The Heated Meeting and Departure
May – September 2025

On May 14, 2025, Star News TV reported that a Martinsville City Council meeting had grown heated, with a sharp exchange unfolding between Mayor LC Jones, Council Member Aaron Rawls, and Hodge himself, in his capacity as HR Director.

"This place has been a powder keg since a certain council member came on board."
— Travis Hodge, HR Director, during the May 14, 2025 council meeting

Star News TV's own coverage raised the question directly: is it appropriate for an HR director to publicly discuss internal workplace dynamics this way? The remark itself doesn't name who Hodge meant by "a certain council member," though the framing of the exchange — Jones, Rawls, and Hodge together — makes Rawls the plausible reference; this record does not treat that as confirmed.

Just over four months later, on September 30, 2025, Star News TV published a "FLASHBACK" post stating plainly: "Former Human Resources Director Travis Hodge no longer works with the City of Martinsville." That is a definitive departure statement, in the past tense, from the same outlet that covered the May incident.

A Separate, Unconfirmed Recollection

Separately, Jordan recalls a different moment from this period: an audience member became involved in a heavy dispute during a council meeting, Police Chief Fincher had to perform what Jordan describes as "room control" to settle the room, and Ferrell-Benavides herself got up and personally escorted the man out of the chamber. Jordan has stated plainly that he assumes this man was Hodge, but never knew him personally and isn't certain. This record cannot confirm whether this is the same May 14 incident viewed from a different moment in the footage, or a separate occurrence entirely. It is documented here exactly as what it is — an honestly flagged, unconfirmed personal recollection — not asserted as established fact.

What The Record Shows

Two things are independently confirmed by the same outlet, months apart: Hodge made a public remark about workplace tension that the press itself questioned as inappropriate for someone in his role, and he subsequently left the city's employ. Whether the remark caused the departure, or the two are simply close in time, isn't established by either post — both are stated as separate facts, and this record doesn't connect them beyond noting the sequence.

Era Three
The Return
Current

Hodge is listed today on the City of Martinsville's own staff directory as Organizational Development Director — an expanded role beyond his prior HR Director title. The city's current official biography describes his service as continuous, from HR Generalist in 2016 through his current title, without mentioning the 2025 departure independently confirmed above.

The Hiring-Practices Audit — Flagged Raises

Separate from the financial purchasing-card audit documented in Chapter 5, Brown Edwards's forensic review also examined the city's hiring and compensation practices specifically. The auditors found that of ten employees hired under Ferrell-Benavides who were tested, nine had no résumé or new-hire documents in their personnel files at all, and that the city could not produce an internal control narrative explaining how interviews, salary decisions, or candidate selection actually worked. Auditors stated plainly: "We could not determine whether or not the City's hiring practices were followed."

Hodge is individually named in this portion of the audit: he received five separate raises between December 2023 and August 2024, totaling approximately $22,000 — a 21.5% increase — attributed to a mix of "interim duties," performance evaluations, and cost-of-living adjustments. The audit's broader finding was that this pattern, across multiple named employees, lacked the documentation needed to establish a legitimate business purpose.

Open Question

The audit doesn't specify which of Hodge's five raises corresponded to which stated justification, or whether any of them coincided with periods when he was serving in an "interim" capacity beyond his HR Director title. This record does not have enough detail to characterize the raises further than the audit's own summary.

Beyond his city role, Hodge holds three confirmed regional institutional positions: he serves as Vice Chair and Secretary of the Harvest Foundation's Grants Committee — one of the region's most significant philanthropic institutions, built from the $200 million Memorial Hospital sale endowment — as Secretary of Martinsville NewCo, a separate 501(c)(3) economic-development nonprofit chaired by Harvest's own board chair Danny Wulff, and as an Executive Board Member of the Martinsville-Henry County United Way. His professional affiliations include the National Forum for Black Public Administrators, the Public Sector Human Resources Association, and the Society for Human Resource Management.

What The Record Shows

Hodge sits on the boards of two separate, overlapping economic-development and philanthropic nonprofits simultaneously, both chaired by the same person (Danny Wulff), in addition to his city role and his United Way seat. That overlap isn't unique to Hodge — it's a recurring pattern across this region's institutional network — but it does mean a single city employee carries real institutional weight well beyond his job title.
