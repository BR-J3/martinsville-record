Aretha Ferrell-Benavides

City Manager, Duncanville TX (2021–2023) → City Manager, Martinsville VA (Oct 2023–Aug 2025) → Federal Plaintiff (2026–Present)

Fired from one city manager job, hired for another, fired again — with a documented pattern of financial-management findings following her from Texas to Virginia. Two leaves, a raise, an office break-in, and a termination that took two votes to finalize. She is now suing the city that fired her.

Era One
Duncanville, Texas
2021 – 2023

Ferrell-Benavides served as City Manager of Duncanville, TX from 2021 to 2023. A Nichols Jackson forensic audit covering her tenure documented a specific pattern that would matter later.

CH3-TX1 Nichols Jackson Duncanville Audit — Key Findings 2023
  • Finance Director not held accountable — complaints dismissed summarily
  • City Manager failed to advise City Council of the gravity of known issues
  • Outside individuals given sensitive financial/budgeting information without Council approval
  • Emails deleted by the City Manager, Finance Director, and Finance Dept employees — presumed public records
  • City Manager approved exceptions to hire unqualified Finance candidates
  • High turnover in Finance, with the City Manager's acquiescence
  • 19 finance concerns and 8 management issues documented overall, tied to her tenure
Source: CH3-TX1 — Nichols Jackson Duncanville Audit, confirmed in hand

The report found no malfeasance, but the pattern was extensive enough that she was fired 4–3 on March 30, 2023 — the same month referenced in the audit's own meeting notes.

What The Record Shows

Stanfield's April 13, 2025 whistleblower letter to Sands Anderson cited financial mismanagement patterns in Martinsville that matched this prior Texas investigation almost exactly. Martinsville hired her in October 2023 — seven months after this report's findings became public in Duncanville.

Era Two
Martinsville City Manager
October 2023 – August 2025

The Four Undisclosed Benefits

Airfare for Jones's Las Vegas trip ($544.71, city card), a hot tub delivered to his residence, an attempted job placement in Petersburg, and a shared attorney for his employment dispute. None disclosed on his Statement of Economic Interests. Full detail in Chapter 2.

The Closed-Session Raise

March 17, 2025: closed session, no legal counsel present, authorized Jones to negotiate her compensation. March 18: Jones signs the offer letter alone. 15% raise, effective April 1.

The Office Break-In

Early February 2025, her own office was broken into and her computer rummaged through. Eric Payne discovered law enforcement hadn't been informed and personally brought it to Police Chief Fincher. A Virginia State Police investigator was brought in.

"Unfortunately, when the investigator arrived, a member of the city manager's staff had erased the computer and all forensic data that would have supported or debunked a computer breach."
— Eric Payne, reported by the Martinsville Bulletin, August 2025

Mayor Jones called for a review of building camera footage and key-card logs. The outcome is unknown. She has said that after this incident she began to fear for her safety.

Open Question

Who erased the computer? Was the camera/key-card review ever conducted? Neither is answered in the public record found so far.

Two Leaves, A Raise In Between

May 21 – June 10, 2025: first leave, cause never publicly disclosed. When pressed by Star News TV, she invoked federal leave protections and brought her personal attorney into the correspondence rather than explain.

Correction to an Earlier Version of This Page

An earlier version of this page stated the raise vote happened July 21, 2025, 4-1, with Jones dissenting. Contemporaneous Cardinal News reporting (published July 24, 2025) confirms this was incorrect on both counts: the vote was July 22, 2025, in a 3-2 decision with Julian Mei and Aaron Rawls dissenting. Jones was part of the majority that approved the raise — consistent with his role signing the March 18 offer letter — not a dissenting vote. Corrected below.

July 22, 2025: council voted 3-2 to raise her salary to $215,000/year, with Julian Mei and Aaron Rawls dissenting.

July 23, 2025: placed on a second, paid administrative leave — barred from city property and contact with employees — tied to a credit card controversy and the authorization of the Brown Edwards forensic audit.

Job-Hunting on the Martinsville Payroll

While collecting her $215,000 salary on leave, she traveled to Elgin, TX to interview for that city's open city manager position — one of four finalists, reportedly engaging more citizens at a meet-and-greet than her competitors. The top candidate declined Elgin's offer August 20, 2025; her status there was uncertain when Martinsville terminated her a week later.

Termination, Twice

August 7, 2025: fired 4-1, Jones dissenting. August 12, 2025: council briefly reverted her status to "suspended" to comply with city charter notice requirements, then finalized the termination again days later.

Era Three
Aftermath / Federal Plaintiff
2025 – Present

She filed an EEOC discrimination complaint before her termination vote.

Contested Claim — Not Adjudicated

Her claim: she has publicly stated that racial tension played a factor in her dismissal.

This Record does not adjudicate that claim. No independent confirmation or rebuttal has been located. It is documented here because she made it, on the record — not because it has been proven or disproven.

Separately, around the same period, Councilman Rayshaun Gravely stated publicly that the investigation's scope extended beyond the city manager alone. This is a different claim from a different person, made at a similar time — it should not be read as corroborating hers.

Contested Claim — Both Sides Shown

Her claim (federal lawsuit, 2026): the entire investigation was retaliatory. She and Jones allege it began only after they questioned Sands Anderson's legal fees, which had increased 245% in a single fiscal year. The suit alleges Sands Anderson "manufactured" the bribery/corruption claims and fed that "work product" to the press, the Commonwealth's Attorney, and the court.

Rawls's direct rebuttal, on the record: "Sands Anderson manufactured nothing. They received disclosures from employees, including the chief financial officer of the city who observed this wrongdoing." This points back to Stanfield's whistleblower letter — already documented in Chapter 1 — as the actual origin.

Both are sworn statements made in active federal litigation. Neither has been adjudicated as of this writing.

The Discrimination Claim, Specifically

Her lawsuit's discrimination count alleges that, at the outset of its investigation, a Sands Anderson attorney asked her directly whether she considered herself racist, and that the firm did not scrutinize white officials in the city with the same level of attention.

A New, More Serious Counter-Claim From Rawls

Her position: as above — the firm's question and its unequal scrutiny are evidence of racial bias in how the investigation was conducted.

Rawls's direct counter, on the record, June 2026: he has stated she "has been making racially-based decisions throughout her tenure," and separately offered this account of something he says she told him personally: "Aretha told me, person to person, it didn't matter what she did or whether it was right or wrong; the only thing that mattered was the city was 52% Black, and she was going to get her way."

This is Rawls's own account of a private, one-on-one conversation. It is not independently corroborated by any other source reviewed, and she has not been asked to respond to this specific characterization in any source found. It is documented here as his sworn/public claim, not as an established fact about anything she actually said.

The Federal Lawsuit

Ferrell-Benavides v. City of Martinsville, Rawls, and Sands Anderson — filed June 9, 2026, WDVA Roanoke, seeking $220,000+ in unpaid severance plus additional damages. Filed the same day as Jones's own suit against Rawls and Sands Anderson, 25 days after CL26-011 was dismissed.

Open Question

Whose account of the Sands Anderson origin — hers and Jones's, or Rawls's — does the eventual Brown Edwards audit support, if either? Not yet resolved by any source found.
