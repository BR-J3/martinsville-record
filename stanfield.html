Person Record · Full Account

J. Richard Stanfield

Chief Financial Officer, City of Martinsville — September 2024 to Present

Almost everything documented elsewhere in this record — the audit, the $10 million in unapproved budget changes, the federal lawsuits — traces back to one CPA's decision to send a confidential letter on a Sunday night from his personal email, after refusing to backdate a federal grant document. This page documents Stanfield's own account, in his own words, from the documents he signed.

Era One
The First Warnings
September 2024 – March 2025

Stanfield is a CPA and CGMA with 30 years of experience. He joined the city as a financial consultant on September 30, 2024, brought in specifically to help complete an overdue annual audit, and was hired as full Chief Financial Officer on January 1, 2025. Media coverage on August 5, 2025 misidentified him as "James Stanfield" — his legal name on every signed document is J. Richard Stanfield, CPA, CGMA; he goes by Richard.

His first formal written warning to city leadership came March 13, 2025 — a signed internal memo to City Manager Aretha Ferrell-Benavides and Chief Operating Officer Edena Reese-Atmore, raising seven distinct concerns.

RS-S1 March 13, 2025 Internal Memo to Ferrell-Benavides & Reese-Atmore Mar 13, 2025
  • 134 credit cards issued across a staff of roughly 350 — recommends limiting cards and using vendor accounts instead
  • Recommends mandatory purchase orders for purchases over $2,000
  • Flags overuse of signature stamps — items rubber-stamped without due attention
  • Timesheet practices inconsistent; flags the Munis Time and Attendance module as too complicated for staff
  • Requests no further changes to account structure until the audit is complete and finances stabilized
  • Flags ARPA risk directly: some projects not encumbered, quarterly filings not done since June 30, 2024 — "this has been previously mentioned and needs to be made a priority"
  • States the prior-year audit is still incomplete: "I'm not talking small amounts, they are all material to the financial statements"
Source: RS-S1 — signed internal memo, confirmed in hand

The memo closes on a notably professional, non-confrontational note, offering continued cooperation rather than escalation.

"I appreciate the faith you have placed in me and stand ready and able to work to address and correct these issues."
— J. Richard Stanfield, closing line, March 13, 2025 memo

One month later, none of the seven points had been meaningfully addressed — and Stanfield had personally discovered something far more serious in the interim: a $4.4 million ARPA obligation deadline the city had already missed.

Era Two
The Whistleblower Letter
April 2025

On Sunday, April 13, 2025, at 10:55 PM, Stanfield sent a confidential letter to Sands Anderson attorney Steve Durbin from his personal Gmail account — not his city email. The cover note alone signals how seriously he took the retaliation risk.

"Martinsville, VA has become Duncanville, Tx 2.0. Please keep this away from the city manager and chief operating officer if you can because they are already being very difficult to deal with and I have no doubts once they are aware of this letter they will do anything in their power to get rid of me or try to destroy my character and reputation."
— J. Richard Stanfield, cover email to Steve Durbin, April 13, 2025, 10:55 PM

The reference to Duncanville was not abstract — Reese-Atmore had previously worked with Ferrell-Benavides there, where, per Stanfield's letter, "issues almost identical to the City of Martinsville happened."

The $4.4 Million Near-Miss

The letter's most consequential disclosure concerned ARPA funding. Stanfield had asked Reese-Atmore about the grant repeatedly from October through December 2024 and was assured it was being handled. He discovered the city had failed to obligate the remaining $4.4 million in ARPA funds by the December 31, 2024 deadline. On March 10, 2025, in a 5:30 PM phone call, Reese-Atmore suggested backdating memoranda of understanding in place of purchase orders to retroactively obligate the funds.

What The Record Shows

Stanfield refused to backdate the documents — by his own account, this would have meant falsifying records tied to federal funds. Instead, on March 31, 2025, he contacted Delegate Eric Phillips directly, who contacted Congressman Morgan Griffith's office, who contacted the U.S. Treasury. On April 7, 2025, Treasury reopened the December 31 reporting window for 24 hours. Stanfield obligated the remaining $4.4 million within that window. By his own description, had he failed, the city would have been required to return the full $4.4 million to the federal government.

The letter also documents that this was not his only finding: the prior year's audit remained incomplete past its legal deadline (Virginia Code requires council approval by December 31 each year); a separate consulting firm's workpapers had been uploaded without review; and Stanfield records Reese-Atmore responding, laughing, that auditors would catch any errors in those workpapers "because she hadn't looked at them."

Retaliation, Already Underway

The letter documents retaliation that preceded its own writing. On March 12, 2025, Stanfield was summoned to the City Manager's office over his "communication style." On March 17, he was summoned again — this time with HR Director Travis Hodge present — and told he wasn't a good fit, was "too negative," and was warned he could be named in the newspaper. Separately, Ferrell-Benavides told him she had stopped texting or emailing because of FOIA exposure.

"I feel my job is at risk and each and every day is a minefield to navigate with constant fear of retribution and termination and assassination of my character."
— J. Richard Stanfield, closing of the April 13, 2025 letter

Era Three
"Open Insubordination"
July 2025

By July 2025, Ferrell-Benavides had formally notified staff that Reese-Atmore was no longer to be involved in finance, budget, or procurement, effective May 20. On July 10, Stanfield discovered Reese-Atmore was still actively adding funds and making changes inside the city's Munis financial system — a day after her access was supposed to have ended.

RS-S3 July 10, 2025 Email Chain — "Clarification Regarding Edena's Involvement" Jul 10, 2025
  • 10:03 AM — Stanfield to Ferrell-Benavides, marked High Importance: Reese-Atmore's access "needs to be removed asap before more damage can be done"
  • 10:15 AM — Ferrell-Benavides replies, CC'ing Jones, Lawson, Fincher, and outside counsel: claims Reese-Atmore is now with a consulting firm completing outstanding tasks
  • 10:32 AM — Stanfield replies: if council is being copied, "let's be fair and include everyone," then lists his own accomplishments accomplished with little support — FY24 audit completed, ARPA fully caught up, FY26 budget loaded
  • 10:34 AM — Ferrell-Benavides, copying HR Director Travis Hodge: "This message is inappropriate and will be documented in your Human Resources File as open insubordination."
Source: RS-S3 — full email chain, confirmed in hand

Stanfield's own message in that chain is measured and factual — a list of completed work, not a personal attack. The "open insubordination" characterization is Ferrell-Benavides's own framing of that message, not an independent finding; it is documented here as part of the exchange, not adopted as accurate.

What The Record Shows

This exchange happened roughly two weeks before Ferrell-Benavides herself was placed on administrative leave on July 23–24, 2025, and about a month before her termination. Everything Stanfield flagged in March and April — the audit delays, the ARPA risk, the credit card controls — became the substance of the forensic audit Brown Edwards later confirmed publicly. The retaliation he predicted in his own letter, down to the specific fear of being formally written up, materialized almost exactly as he described it.

Beyond the City Role

Stanfield holds two confirmed institutional positions separate from his city CFO role: he serves as Treasurer of the Dream Center of Martinsville and Henry County, a transitional-housing ministry that partners with Virginia corrections, probation, and jail systems — directly linking the city's own financial oversight to the region's reentry infrastructure — and he is a confirmed member of the Henry County Community Policy Management Team (CPMT), a joint Henry County/Martinsville body overseeing Children's Services Act funding for at-risk youth. Both positions place the city's subpoenaed CFO inside two separate city/county institutional bodies, on top of the financial oversight role already documented above.
