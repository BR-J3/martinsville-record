Person Record · Limited Account

Steve Durbin

Attorney, Sands Anderson — Martinsville's Outside City Counsel Since April 2023

The attorney who received Stanfield's whistleblower letter, and the one who repeatedly asked Mayor Jones to recuse himself from a closed session — and was refused. His own firm's role sat at a structural conflict from the start: simultaneously advising the council and conducting the forensic audit examining the council's own city manager. This page is shorter than most on this Record, because the public record on Durbin personally is thinner than most.

Era One
The Dual Role
April 2023 – Present

Sands Anderson, the Richmond-based law firm, has served as Martinsville's outside city attorney through Steve Durbin specifically since April 2023. That role placed him in a structural position this record has already flagged elsewhere: the same firm simultaneously held the forensic-audit relationship and the general advise-the-council relationship — two functions that, depending on what the audit found, could pull in different directions for the same client.

Open Question

No biographical detail on Durbin beyond his role and firm has been located in any source reviewed — no bar admission year, no prior career history, no public statements outside the two specific episodes documented below. This page is built entirely from his documented actions in this case, not from any independent profile of him.

That dual-role structure is the same one now at the center of active litigation: both LC Jones and Aretha Ferrell-Benavides allege in their June 9, 2026 federal suits that Sands Anderson's billing to the city increased 245% in a single fiscal year, and that the firm's investigation became retaliatory only after they began questioning that increase. Full detail on those suits, and Rawls's direct rebuttal, is in Chapter 9 and on Jones's and Ferrell-Benavides's own pages — this page does not repeat that dispute, since Durbin is not personally named as a defendant in either case; Sands Anderson the firm is.

What The Record Shows

Durbin himself has not been named as a defendant, has not made any public statement found in this record, and has not been asked to respond to anything documented below. Whatever happens to Sands Anderson's relationship with the city — a separate, currently unconfirmed matter tracked elsewhere in this record — is not the same question as what Durbin personally did in the two specific, dated episodes that follow.

Era Two
The Letter and The Recusal Fight
April 2025 – November 2025

Durbin is the named recipient of the single most consequential document in this entire record. On Sunday, April 13, 2025, at 10:55 PM, CFO J. Richard Stanfield sent his confidential whistleblower letter to Durbin from his personal Gmail account — not his city email, and addressed specifically to Durbin by name, not to the firm generally. That letter is what initiated the investigation that produced the forensic audit, the prosecution, and effectively everything else chronicled in this record. Full detail: Chapter 1 and Stanfield's own page.

RS-S2 Stanfield Whistleblower Letter — Addressed to Durbin Apr 13, 2025
  • Sent to Durbin personally, by name, at Sands Anderson — not a general firm inquiry
  • Stanfield's cover note asked Durbin to keep the letter from Ferrell-Benavides and Reese-Atmore if possible, citing fear of retaliation
  • No record found of Durbin's specific response to Stanfield, or of what action he personally took on receiving it, beyond the investigation it triggered
Source: RS-S2, confirmed in hand. See Stanfield's own page for the full letter.

Seven months later, Durbin appears again — this time in person, in conflict with the mayor directly. Per Aaron Rawls's notarized affidavit, on November 12, 2025, four council members convened with Sands Anderson and Woods Rogers attorneys for a closed session on the forensic audit's preliminary findings and Ferrell-Benavides's EEOC complaint. Jones entered the session at approximately 6:45 PM despite being told not to, and refused repeated recusal requests from Durbin — causing the Woods Rogers attorneys present to decline giving EEOC counsel with Jones in the room.

What The Record Shows

This is Rawls's sworn account of what he witnessed, not independently confirmed by a second source, and Jones has not been asked to respond to this specific characterization in any source found. Within the limits of that single source, though, it shows Durbin in the more active of two possible postures: not a passive recipient of information, but someone willing to directly and repeatedly ask the city's own mayor to leave a room — and being refused.

Open Question

Nothing in any source reviewed documents what happened after Jones refused — whether Durbin escalated the refusal, whether the session proceeded anyway with Jones present, or whether any formal complaint was filed over it. The affidavit records the refusal itself, not its aftermath.
