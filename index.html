The Short Version
Ten things that happened in Martinsville. All of them documented.
  • A city manager gave the mayor a hot tub, paid for his Las Vegas trip, tried to get him a job, and arranged him a lawyer. None of it was disclosed.
  • The mayor signed her a 15% pay raise — alone, in secret, with no legal counsel present. One signature. No other council member knew.
  • A council member who publicly opposed the raise was physically removed from chambers by a deputy sheriff acting on a nonverbal signal — while the raise had already been signed seven days earlier.
  • The city's own CFO blew the whistle — first internally, then in a formal letter to the city's outside lawyers.
  • A forensic audit found $10 million in budget changes entered without council approval, including a six-figure transfer from the city's own economic development authority. The findings are public; a separate workplace investigation is still being held.
  • Citizens organized a petition drive to remove the mayor. Three officials gave three different signature thresholds. The court ruled a number nearly double what petitioners had been told they needed.
  • The mayor was suspended, then reinstated. Multiple special prosecutors handled the case. The Virginia Attorney General declined, citing a procedural technicality.
  • The removal case was dismissed with prejudice on May 15, 2026 — not on the merits, but on signatures. A separate workplace investigation report remains unreleased.
  • Two new federal lawsuits were filed 25 days after the dismissal. The fired city manager is suing for $220,000+ in unpaid severance. The city — meaning taxpayers — is a named defendant.
  • While still suspended and awaiting jury trial, the mayor announced he will seek reelection. A separate workplace investigation report has not been publicly released. The Virginia State Police investigation is ongoing. The record continues.
Every item above is sourced to a named document, a sworn affidavit, a court filing, or a city record. The chapters below contain the full documented record.
What This Is

This is a sourced, chapter-by-chapter public record of how Martinsville, Virginia's city government arrived at $10 million in unauthorized budget amendments, a forensic audit, multiple federal lawsuits, a mayor's suspension, a special prosecutor chain, and an active Virginia State Police investigation — and who did what, in what order, with what documented consequences.

Every claim in these chapters traces to a named document, a named source, or a public record. No editorializing. The sequence says it. The public record does the work.
