Chapter Five

The Audits

Two Documents, Not One · One Released · One Still Held

This record held this chapter for months waiting on a single audit. There were always two. The financial forensic audit has been substantially public since April 14, 2026, with real findings now on record. A separate workplace investigation into the same former city manager has been delayed repeatedly since August 2025, and was most recently pushed to a planned July 14, 2026 release the very week Mayor Jones returned to council. This chapter documents both, precisely separated.

Financial AuditBrown Edwards — Released Apr 14, 2026
Workplace InvestigationSands Anderson — Holds for Jul 14, 2026
VSP InvestigationActive, separate, ongoing
The Record

For months, this record treated "the audit" as a single document — sealed, expected sometime in mid-July 2026. That framing was wrong, and worth correcting plainly rather than quietly: there are two separate documents, conducted by different parties, examining different things, on two different release timelines. Conflating them made it impossible to report accurately on either one. This chapter separates them.

A Note On Attribution

One news report from January 2026 describes the financial audit as "conducted by the law firm Sands Anderson," while the actual April 14, 2026 release is explicitly attributed to accounting firm Brown Edwards. The most plausible read is that Sands Anderson, the city's retained law firm, coordinated multiple workstreams — the financial audit, the separate workplace investigation below, and general legal counsel — while Brown Edwards performed the actual forensic accounting. That is this record's best read of the available reporting, not a confirmed fact, and is presented as such.

Part One
The Financial Audit
Brown Edwards · Released April 14, 2026

The financial forensic audit examined Aretha Ferrell-Benavides's use of a city-issued purchasing card from February 2024 through June 2025 — 307 separate purchases in total. Brown Edwards completed the audit and delivered it to council around November 2025. It sat internally for roughly five months before any public release. On March 30, 2026, council voted 5-0 to take a formal step toward releasing it. On April 14, 2026, council voted to release it, while Jones remained suspended and former mayor Michael Teague held his seat.

After Brown Edwards completed the audit, then-Commonwealth's Attorney Andy Hall referred its findings to a special prosecutor, Bedford County's Wes Nance — the same referral documented on Hall's person-page. That referral was a direct response to what this specific audit found, not a separately initiated criminal inquiry.

What The Audit Found

CH5-AUDIT Brown Edwards Forensic Audit — Key Findings Apr 2026
  • 307 purchases reviewed on Ferrell-Benavides's city purchasing card, Feb 2024 – June 2025
  • 68 of those 307 — about 22 percent — were never reviewed by anyone besides Ferrell-Benavides herself; the remainder were approved by an employee reporting directly to her, not an impartial official
  • $96,613 spent without proper approval, per one published summary of the audit's findings
  • Separately reported: more than $15,000 in charges where auditors could find no business purpose at all
  • Overspent the $45/day meal per diem by $1,730 across 16 months; overspent hotel budget by $18,409
  • Cited examples: a lunch exceeding $100 at Fontaine Blue in Las Vegas; a Washington, DC Marriott stay exceeding $2,000
  • Travel was not approved in advance until April 2025, a violation of city code requiring prior approval
  • $169,180 transferred from the Martinsville-Henry County Economic Development Corporation to help fund City Manager's Office salaries and travel, without that department's input
  • 247 additional purchases by other employees reviewed separately; auditors found it "impossible to conclude whether the transactions tested have a legitimate business purpose" for many of them
  • Audit's own language: "inadequate or non-existent internal controls," transactions "in direct violation of the City's purchasing card policy"
Source: Cardinal News, WSET, WDBJ7, RealClearInvestigations/WND, confirmed in hand
"There is no crime. There is no fraud. There is no money missing. There is no bribery."
— Paul Goldman, attorney for Aretha Ferrell-Benavides, on the audit's findings

Goldman called the process a "witch hunt," and said Ferrell-Benavides was not given an opportunity to respond to the findings and was not contacted during the review. He noted that not all of the flagged charges were personally connected to her.

"It was the worst aspects of government I expected to see, and they were there."
— Councilman Aaron Rawls, on the audit's findings

Rawls said he had received complaints from staff about travel expenses and a lack of oversight well before the audit's release. Current City Manager Robert Fincher said safeguards are now in place — including the purchasing-card elimination already documented on his person-page — to prevent similar spending going forward.

What The Record Shows

The financial audit's findings are now public and substantiated by multiple independent outlets: undisclosed personal-feeling spending, minimal internal review, and a six-figure inter-departmental transfer that funded a salary increase without the affected department's input. Ferrell-Benavides's own attorney does not dispute the spending occurred — he disputes that it amounts to a crime. Whether it does remains a separate, open question, tied to the still-active VSP investigation below, not to anything in this audit itself.

Part Two
The Workplace Investigation
Sands Anderson · Holds for July 14, 2026

Separately, Sands Anderson conducted a workplace investigation into Ferrell-Benavides's conduct as city manager — distinct from the financial audit above, and the document this record was actually waiting on, even when it was filed under the wrong name. This is the report whose July 14–24, 2026 hold date has anchored this record's publishing strategy for months.

A Pattern Of Delay

August 2025
First Release Vote Fails
A vote to release the workplace investigation fails 2-2, with Jones abstaining.
January 2026
Rawls and Jones Clash Publicly
Jones opposes release, citing risk of defamation suits against city employees named in the report. Rawls accuses Jones of being "conflicted... in writing," and calls Jones a crude name during the meeting.
February 2026
Jones Suspended
Separately, Jones is suspended from office pending the CL26-011 removal trial (Chapter 7).
March 30, 2026
Closed Session Added
Council adds a closed session specifically to discuss the workplace investigation. No indication given that findings will be made public.
April 21, 2026
Jones Reinstated
Circuit court ends Jones's suspension (Chapter 7).
April 23, 2026
Rawls Predicts The Delay
"I don't think we're going to have it coming out now because you have three bad people in the majority. Again, we were only able to get out the first investigation because you had a decent person filling in for the mayor."
April 28, 2026
Jones's First Meeting Back
Council goes into closed session again on the workplace investigation. Jones steps out of portions of the session himself.
April 28–29, 2026
Council Votes To Postpone Until July 14
Same meeting where a roughly $4 million budget deficit and proposed tax increases were announced. Rawls, separately: "You supported fools, and your taxes are going up. Congratulations."
What The Record Shows

Five days before the postponement vote, Rawls predicted almost exactly what happened — explicitly tying it to Jones's reinstatement. Whether that reflects genuine foresight, a self-fulfilling public pressure campaign, or simply an accurate read of where the council's votes stood, this record does not resolve. The prediction and the outcome are both on the record, in that order.

The Virginia State Police Investigation

Separate from both documents above, the Virginia State Police opened a criminal investigation into Martinsville governance matters, tied to the same spending allegations against Ferrell-Benavides. As of the most recent public confirmation, the investigation remains active.

VSP Investigation — Active, Timeline Uncertain

"A Virginia State Police investigation is ongoing. How or when that will end is uncertain." This record's last confirmed public update on the VSP investigation's status is April 21, 2026, via Patti Covington's statement (Chapter 7). No charges have been announced as of this publication.

What's Still Sealed

The financial audit's published findings are summary-level reporting on the city's prepared materials, not the complete underlying record. The full 1.6 gigabytes of supporting documentation — receipts, full ledgers, and related backup files — remains the subject of a denied FOIA request, separate from the summary findings now public. Whether that underlying documentation is released alongside or separately from the workplace investigation in July is not yet known.
