Julian Mei

Martinsville City Council Member — 2022–Present

Not a central figure in the conduct documented elsewhere in this record. That absence is itself worth noting: Mei's documented role across this entire case is consistently procedural — defending citizens' rights to petition, present at the November 12 closed session, and the person Jones approached on January 8, 2026. This page reflects what the record actually shows, including where it stays quiet.

The Record

The Petition — Defending Process, Not Taking a Side on Outcome

When the citizen petition to remove Jones launched in December 2025, Mei was quoted publicly:

"It doesn't really matter if I support it, or don't support it. Citizens have a right to have a say in their government, and that includes the lawful process to remove people from office, if they deem it necessary. The same would apply to me and any other elected official."
— Councilman Julian Mei, quoted in Star News TV coverage, December 2025
What The Record Shows

Mei's statement defends the process of citizen accountability without endorsing the petition's outcome — a deliberately neutral, procedural position. He applied the same standard to himself ("the same would apply to me") as to Jones.

November 12, 2025 — Closed Session

Mei was one of four council members (alongside Gravely, Lawson, and Rawls) present for the closed session reviewing the forensic audit's preliminary findings and Ferrell-Benavides's EEOC complaint, per Rawls's sworn affidavit. Mei did not himself recommend or initiate the session's most contested action — Jones's unrequested entry and refusal to recuse — and the affidavit does not attribute any disputed conduct to Mei in this session.

January 8, 2026 — The Confrontation

Per Rawls's affidavit, Jones approached Mei during a public council meeting; law enforcement intervened. Mei, who has a documented panic disorder he has publicly addressed, required assistance leaving chambers. The Martinsville/Henry County Democratic Committee separately and publicly rebuked commenter Melody Cartwright for mocking Mei's mental health afterward — confirming both the condition and the community's defense of him are part of the independent public record, not only Rawls's account. Full detail on this incident: Jones's and Rawls's pages.

The NCI Closed-Door Meeting — June 2025

Mei was removed from a closed-door Sorensen Institute meeting at the New College Institute, alongside journalist Luis Romero, while Jones, Ferrell-Benavides, Fincher, and Vice Mayor Lawson remained. No agenda was provided. Neither Mei nor Romero has publicly addressed the removal in the year since. Full detail: the NCI research thread.

Open Question

Why was a sitting council member removed from a meeting attended by the city's top officials? Mei has not commented publicly. Not resolved.

Mei and Gravely were separated by only 8 votes. Lawson, Mei, and Gravely won the three open at-large seats in this election together and have since served alongside each other through the entirety of the events documented in this record. Their terms run through 2028 — distinct from Jones's and Rawls's 2022-elected seats, which are on the November 2026 ballot.

What The Record Shows, Overall

Mei does not appear in this record as a central actor in the undisclosed benefits, the raise, the financial misconduct, or the legal disputes documented elsewhere. His documented role is consistently procedural: defending the petition process without taking a side, present but not implicated at the November 12 session, and the target rather than the source of confrontation on January 8. If further research surfaces a more active role, this page will be updated — but as of this writing, the record shows restraint, not involvement, and that absence is itself a documented fact worth recording plainly.
