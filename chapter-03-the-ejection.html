Chapter Three

The Ejection

March 25 · Keen · The Nod · The Pay Raise Letter

On March 25, 2025, Martinsville City Council Member Aaron Rawls was removed from a public council meeting by a deputy sheriff — while speaking against a pay raise that had already been secretly signed seven days earlier. Two video angles exist. The city manager has given three different accounts of what happened.

DateMarch 25, 2025
LocationMartinsville City Council Chambers
ActorDeputy Sheriff Reva Keen
Offer Letter SignedMarch 18, 2025
The Record

At a Martinsville City Council meeting on March 25, 2025, Council Member Aaron Rawls was speaking against a proposed pay raise for City Manager Aretha Ferrell-Benavides. He did not know — and had no way of knowing — that the pay raise had already been signed into effect seven days earlier, on March 18, 2025, under the sole signature of Mayor LC Jones.

While Rawls was speaking, Deputy Sheriff Reva Keen removed him from the chambers. What follows is the sequence as documented across two video sources and multiple public statements — including three separate, evolving accounts from the city manager about what happened.

What Was Already Done — March 18, 2025

Seven days before the March 25 council meeting, on March 17, 2025, the Martinsville City Council met in closed session. No legal counsel was present. During that session, Mayor Jones was authorized to negotiate Ferrell-Benavides's compensation.

The following day — March 18, 2025 — Jones signed an offer letter on official City of Martinsville letterhead. The letter granted Ferrell-Benavides a 15% compensation increase effective April 1, 2025. The letter bears Jones's sole signature. No other council member co-signed it.

What The Record Shows

On March 25, 2025, Aaron Rawls stood before the Martinsville City Council and publicly opposed a pay raise for the city manager. The raise had already been signed — by the mayor alone, in a closed session with no legal counsel, seven days earlier. Rawls was removed from the chambers before he finished speaking. He would not learn the raise had already been approved until approximately six weeks later.

The Meeting — March 25, 2025

Rawls had requested time to "reclaim a few minutes" he said he'd previously yielded in prior meetings, telling the council, "I really want to talk about real issues... I have a lot I want to discuss." His comments followed a closed session the week before in which senior staff pay had been discussed amid an ongoing property tax reassessment — a session Rawls had been unable to attend.

As Rawls spoke, Mayor Jones repeatedly instructed him: "Fall in order." Rawls responded that he did not understand the instruction. The exchange repeated several times, with Jones gaveling and members of the audience reportedly echoing "fall in order" along with him. Jones then stated Rawls was "making personal attacks" and said something to the effect of moving on, at which point Deputy Sheriff Reva Keen began approaching Rawls. The audience clapped and cheered as Keen stood beside Rawls to remove him. Jones gaveled again, repeating "we're moving on," after Keen was already standing beside Rawls — not before. Jones then turned the floor to Councilman Julian Mei, who continued the meeting while Rawls was escorted out.

Two Camera Angles, Three Statements

The official council livestream captured the meeting from the dais. A second video, recorded from a different angle in the chambers, was published by Caleb Robertson of WDTBS and shared by Star News TV on March 27, 2025 under the headline "Who gave the Martinsville City Sheriff's Deputy the order to Extract Councilman Aaron Rawls from the City Council Meeting — You be the JUDGE!" The post drew significant public engagement at the time.

During the exchange in which Jones stated Rawls was "making personal attacks," Ferrell-Benavides is visible turning toward the deputy and making a sideways head motion. What that motion meant has been the subject of three separate public statements from Ferrell-Benavides since:

"No one signed the deputy."
— Aretha Ferrell-Benavides, email statement, reported by BTW21 (Beyond the Walls), late March 2025
"No one had this magic plan to do anything evil, and the only person who has the authority over and run the meeting is the mayor... The exchange went back and forth and he hit his gavel, if you watch the video and said can you move on?"
— Aretha Ferrell-Benavides, reported by WSET, March 2025
"...I saw my head nod, I'm not sure who to, but with all the tension, it could have been anyone."
— Aretha Ferrell-Benavides, statement to the Martinsville Bulletin, reported by Star News TV, March 29, 2025
What The Record Shows

Ferrell-Benavides's account of the nod has shifted across three separate public statements: first that no one signed the deputy at all; then that only the mayor has authority over the meeting and that he gaveled before the removal; then that her own head nod did occur, while disputing who it was directed toward. All three statements are on the public record. They are not fully consistent with one another. Separately, at the same press conference where this dispute became public, Rawls and Councilman Julian Mei stated the removal was "a coordinated effort between City Manager Aretha Ferrell-Benavides and the other three council members" — a broader claim than the city manager acting alone, made publicly, two days after the ejection.

Reva Keen — Deputy Sheriff, Martinsville

Reva Keen served as a Martinsville Deputy Sheriff assigned to provide security at city council meetings. The Rawls federal complaint — Case 4:25-cv-00028, filed June 2, 2025 — names Keen as a defendant. The complaint alleges that Keen's removal of Rawls violated his First Amendment rights and constituted unlawful retaliation for his protected speech as an elected official.

The Press Conference — March 27, 2025

Two days after the ejection, Rawls and Mei held a press conference. Mei stated: "I cannot think of a more disturbing experience occurring in the history of Martinsville, maybe the commonwealth itself... Aaron's rights of speech under the First Amendment have been violated." Rawls stated: "Notice that no one thought about removing me until I started criticizing pay raises for executives. That was the thing that got me removed."

The Pay Raise Form — Contemporaneous Documentation

The March 18 offer letter was not the only document. An Employee Action and Change Form was also completed, formalizing Ferrell-Benavides's 15% compensation increase effective April 1, 2025. Both documents existed and were in effect on March 25 — the day Rawls was removed for opposing the raise.

April 30, 2025 — Rawls Learns From the News

Aaron Rawls learned that the pay raise had already been signed — not from the mayor, not from the city manager, not from any official notification — but from a news media report, on or about April 30, 2025. That is approximately six weeks after the offer letter was signed, and five weeks after it took effect.

The Timeline Gap

March 17: Closed session authorizes Jones to negotiate. March 18: Jones signs offer letter alone. April 1: Raise takes effect. March 25: Rawls removed from chambers while opposing the raise. April 30: Rawls learns via news media the raise was already done. The city did not notify him.

A council member was removed from public chambers while speaking against an action that had already been secretly completed. Two video angles of the moment exist. The city manager has given three separate accounts of what her own gesture meant. The council member learned the raise had already happened from a newspaper. These are the documented facts of March 25, 2025.
