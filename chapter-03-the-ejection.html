Chapter Three

The Ejection

March 25 · Keen · The Nod · The Pay Raise Letter

On March 25, 2025, Martinsville City Council Member Aaron Rawls was removed from a public council meeting by a deputy sheriff acting on a nonverbal signal — while speaking against a pay raise that had already been secretly signed seven days earlier.

The Record

At a Martinsville City Council meeting on March 25, 2025, Council Member Aaron Rawls was speaking against a proposed pay raise for City Manager Aretha Ferrell-Benavides. He did not know — and had no way of knowing — that the pay raise had already been signed into effect seven days earlier, on March 18, 2025, under the sole signature of Mayor LC Jones.

While Rawls was speaking, Deputy Sheriff Reva Keen moved to remove him from the chambers. No verbal order was issued by the mayor. No rule of order was cited. The documented record indicates Keen acted on a nonverbal signal from Ferrell-Benavides.

Rawls learned the pay raise had already been signed approximately six weeks later — from a news media report, not from the city.

What Was Already Done — March 18, 2025

Seven days before the March 25 council meeting, on March 17, 2025, the Martinsville City Council met in closed session. No legal counsel was present. During that session, Mayor Jones was authorized to negotiate Ferrell-Benavides's compensation.

The following day — March 18, 2025 — Jones signed an offer letter on official City of Martinsville letterhead. The letter granted Ferrell-Benavides a 15% compensation increase effective April 1, 2025. The letter bears Jones's sole signature. No other council member co-signed it.

What The Record Shows

On March 25, 2025, Aaron Rawls stood before the Martinsville City Council and publicly opposed a pay raise for the city manager. The raise had already been signed — by the mayor alone, in a closed session with no legal counsel, seven days earlier. Rawls was removed from the chambers before he finished speaking. He would not learn the raise had already been approved until approximately six weeks later.

The Meeting — March 25, 2025

At the March 25 council meeting, Rawls addressed the proposed compensation increase for Ferrell-Benavides during public proceedings. His opposition was on the record — a council member, in chambers, speaking against a measure in the normal course of city business.

While he was speaking, Deputy Sheriff Reva Keen approached and removed Rawls from the chambers. The removal was not preceded by a verbal order from the presiding officer. No specific rule violation was cited in real time. The documented record in the Rawls federal complaint and sworn affidavit establishes that Keen acted on a nonverbal signal from Ferrell-Benavides — not on an order from the mayor or any council member with authority to direct removal.

Reva Keen — Deputy Sheriff, Martinsville

Reva Keen served as a Martinsville Deputy Sheriff assigned to provide security at city council meetings. Her role was to maintain order when directed by the presiding officer under established rules of procedure.

The Rawls federal complaint — Case 4:25-cv-00028, filed June 2, 2025 — names Keen as a defendant. The complaint alleges that Keen's removal of Rawls violated his First Amendment rights and constituted unlawful retaliation for his protected speech as an elected official.

The Pay Raise Form — Contemporaneous Documentation

The March 18 offer letter was not the only document. An Employee Action and Change Form was also completed, formalizing Ferrell-Benavides's 15% compensation increase effective April 1, 2025. Both documents existed and were in effect on March 25 — the day Rawls was removed for opposing the raise.

  • Effect: Formalizes 15% compensation increase for Ferrell-Benavides
  • Effective date: April 1, 2025
  • Companion to the March 18 offer letter
  • Both documents confirmed in hand
Source: CH2-B — Employee Action Form, confirmed in hand

April 30, 2025 — Rawls Learns From the News

Aaron Rawls learned that the pay raise had already been signed — not from the mayor, not from the city manager, not from any official notification — but from a news media report, on or about April 30, 2025. That is approximately six weeks after the offer letter was signed, and five weeks after it took effect.

The Timeline Gap

March 17: Closed session authorizes Jones to negotiate. March 18: Jones signs offer letter alone, no other council member present or co-signing. April 1: Raise takes effect. March 25: Rawls removed from chambers while opposing the raise. April 30: Rawls learns via news media the raise was already done. The city did not notify him.

A council member was removed from public chambers while speaking against an action that had already been secretly completed. The officer who removed him acted on a signal from the person who stood to benefit from the action being opposed. The council member learned what had happened from a newspaper. These are the documented facts of March 25, 2025.
