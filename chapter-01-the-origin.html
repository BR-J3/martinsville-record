Whistleblower · ARPA · Duncanville, TX Pattern

The Record

Every governance crisis has a moment when someone inside the institution decides to put something in writing. In Martinsville, that moment came on April 13, 2025, when City Chief Financial Officer J. Richard Stanfield sent a letter to Sands Anderson — the law firm serving as Martinsville's city attorney.

Stanfield was not a political opponent. He was not a community activist or outside agitator. He was the city's own CFO — a CPA with direct access to every financial transaction flowing through Martinsville's accounts. What he put in writing that day set every subsequent event in motion.

But to understand what Stanfield was describing, you have to go back further — to a city in Texas that had already lived this story.

Duncanville, Texas — March 20, 2024

Aretha Ferrell-Benavides served as City Manager of Duncanville, Texas from 2021 to 2023. She left that position before taking the Martinsville city manager role in October 2023.

On March 20, 2024 — while Ferrell-Benavides was already serving as Martinsville's city manager — the firm Nichols Jackson released a forensic audit report on the City of Duncanville covering her tenure there. The report was eight pages. Its findings were specific and documented.

What The Record Shows

A forensic audit documenting financial irregularities under Ferrell-Benavides's management was released publicly on March 20, 2024 — while she was serving as Martinsville's city manager. Martinsville hired her in October 2023 citing her prior city management experience, including her Duncanville tenure.

Inside Martinsville — The CFO Memo

On March 13, 2025, Stanfield sent an internal memo to Ferrell-Benavides and Assistant City Manager Reese-Atmore. The memo flagged specific financial concerns internally before Stanfield escalated the matter outside the city manager's chain of command.

CH1-C Stanfield Internal Memo — to Ferrell-Benavides and Reese-Atmore Mar 13, 2025
  • Author: J. Richard Stanfield, Chief Finance Officer, City of Martinsville
  • Recipients: Aretha Ferrell-Benavides (City Manager) and Reese-Atmore (Assistant City Manager)
  • Internal document — sent within city management chain
  • Preceded the external whistleblower letter by exactly 31 days
  • Stanfield attempted internal resolution before escalating externally
Source: CH1-C — Stanfield Internal Memo, confirmed in hand

The internal memo went nowhere. Thirty-one days later, Stanfield went outside the city manager's chain entirely.

April 13, 2025 — The Letter

On April 13, 2025, J. Richard Stanfield sent a formal letter to Sands Anderson — the law firm serving as Martinsville's city attorney. By addressing the letter to the city's outside legal counsel rather than city management, Stanfield was documenting concerns that he believed city management would not or could not address internally.

CH1-A Stanfield Whistleblower Letter to Sands Anderson Apr 13, 2025
  • Author: J. Richard Stanfield, CFO, City of Martinsville
  • Recipient: Sands Anderson law firm — city's outside legal counsel
  • Date: April 13, 2025
  • Sent 31 days after internal memo to Ferrell-Benavides produced no resolution
  • Documented specific financial irregularities under city manager's tenure
  • Triggered Sands Anderson investigation into city financial practices
  • Stanfield subpoenaed to testify in CL26-011
Source: CH1-A — Stanfield Letter, confirmed in hand
What The Record Shows

Martinsville's own CFO attempted internal resolution on March 13, 2025. When that produced no result, he sent a formal letter to the city's outside legal counsel on April 13, 2025. That letter is what initiated the Sands Anderson investigation — the investigation that ultimately produced the forensic audit, the removal petition, and the chain of events documented in the chapters that follow.

The ARPA Connection

Among the financial irregularities Stanfield was tracking: the handling of American Rescue Plan Act funds — federal COVID relief money distributed to municipalities beginning in 2021. ARPA funds carried strict federal guidelines on eligible uses, reporting requirements, and documentation standards.

Martinsville received ARPA funding during Ferrell-Benavides's tenure as city manager, which began October 2023. The city was required to report ARPA expenditures to the US Treasury. Stanfield's role as CFO put him directly in the position of certifying those reports — and identifying when expenditures did not align with required documentation.

The Audit — Still Sealed

The Brown Edwards forensic audit exists. The city confirmed 1.6 gigabytes of records when denying a FOIA request. The public has received a summary only. The full audit is withheld under Virginia Code 2.2-3705.1 — active litigation exemption. Full release expected July 14–24, 2026. Chapter 5 publishes when it drops.
