Rayshaun Gravely

Martinsville City Council Member — 2022–Present · Term expires 2028

A felony charge reduced to a misdemeanor before conviction. A name spelled differently across different eras of his public record. A council vote where he sided with Jones and personally paid out of pocket anyway. This page documents Gravely's record precisely — correcting an inaccurate community claim along the way, not just repeating it.

The Record

The Felony Reduction — Case GC22001064-00

Court records confirm: filed April 20, 2022, offense date June 6, 2021. Original charge: Strangle/Wound/Injury, Va. Code §18.2-51.6 — a Class 6 felony. The charge was amended to Disorderly Conduct under §18.2-415, a misdemeanor. Gravely pleaded nolo contendere; the disposition was guilty on the amended charge. Sentence: 90 days, fully suspended, plus $250 in fines and $101 in costs, paid February 20, 2025. The complainant is named in court records as T.J. Brown.

Correcting a Common Community Claim

A frequent public criticism holds that Gravely "should have lost his voting rights" given the original felony charge. This is not factually accurate as stated. Virginia law strips voting rights upon felony conviction — and Gravely was never convicted of a felony in this case. The charge was amended to a misdemeanor before conviction, which is precisely why no voting-rights consequence applies. The accurate, and arguably stronger, version of this concern is different: the original charge was serious — felony-level, alleging strangulation and wounding — and the case resolved as a misdemeanor with a fully suspended sentence. That is a significant reduction in severity, not a circumvention of the law. This record states the precise version because it holds up to scrutiny; the imprecise version does not.

Andy Hall's Office Handled the Case

Andy Hall served as Martinsville Commonwealth's Attorney continuously from December 2017 through January 2026, when he announced he would not seek reelection. That means Hall's office held jurisdiction throughout 2022, when Gravely's felony charge was reduced and resolved. This confirms Hall's office handled the matter — it does not confirm Hall personally argued or signed off on this specific case, since Commonwealth's Attorneys routinely delegate individual cases to deputies.

What The Record Shows

Andy Hall is not a new name in this record. He cited the disputed 571-signature petition threshold (Chapter 6) and is a named defendant in the Mook v. Hall federal case. Hall's office handling Gravely's felony reduction three years before the petition dispute adds a third, earlier connection — the same office, under Hall and his successor Patrick Flinn, touches both the Gravely matter and the Jones removal case.

Open Question

Court records show inconsistent name spellings across filings ("Rayshaun," "Rayshawn," "O'Shea," "Oshea," "Junio," "Jumon"). One observation from this research, not yet independently verified: one spelling format may have been used consistently during Gravely's prior employment as a Henry County Sheriff's Deputy, with a different format appearing in his current council-era records. This pattern has not been confirmed against dated records from each period and should not be treated as established until it is. Separately, a criminal defense attorney based on Church Street, Martinsville, is believed — not yet confirmed — to have represented Gravely during these 2021–2023 proceedings.

Caution: Henry County's court index lists several unrelated Gravely family members (Orlando Marquis, Pryor Deshazo, Ronald Keith, Ryan Marquis, Romante Novarius, Shakina Lavette) with entirely separate cases. Only Rayshaun O'Shea-variant filings are attributed to the council member on this page.

The NAACP Funding Vote — November 5, 2025

Council deadlocked 2-2 on a motion to fund the local NAACP chapter's community outreach programs. Gravely voted for the funding, alongside Mayor Jones. Rawls and Mei voted against. Vice Mayor Kathy Lawson was absent, which created the deadlock.

CH6-M1 NAACP Funding Vote — Aftermath Nov 5, 2025
  • Motion failed 2-2: Jones and Gravely for; Rawls and Mei against; Lawson absent
  • Despite voting against city funding, Rawls and Mei each personally pledged $500 to the NAACP
  • Jones personally pledged $500 as well
  • Combined $1,500 in personal donations followed the failed public funding vote
Source: Star News TV, November 5, 2025, confirmed in hand
What The Record Shows

This is the same Jones/Gravely versus Rawls/Mei factional split visible throughout the broader governance crisis — but the aftermath cuts against a simple villain narrative. All three council members present found their own way to support the same cause personally, regardless of how they voted publicly. That doesn't erase the rest of this record. It's simply also true, and belongs in it.

The Affidavit — A Different Role Than Jones's

Rawls's sworn affidavit places Gravely in two specific moments. On May 13, 2025, when Jones approached Rawls aggressively during a public meeting, Gravely physically intercepted Jones before police arrived. On November 12, 2025, Gravely was one of four council members present for the closed session on the forensic audit's preliminary findings. Neither moment attributes disputed conduct to Gravely himself — in the May 13 incident specifically, the affidavit describes him acting to de-escalate, not provoke.
