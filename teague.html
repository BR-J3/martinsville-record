Michael Gene Teague

City Council 1994–2018, Mayor 2002–2018 · Court-Appointed Council Member, Feb–Apr 2026

Eight years after leaving office voluntarily to "give someone else a chance," Gene Teague was called back to the same council chambers by a circuit court judge — not to run for anything, but to occupy a vacant seat during the most contested suspension in the city's modern history. He accepted, and declined to be paid for it.

Era One
The Mayor
1994 – 2018

Teague — a University of Virginia graduate who built his career in textiles management at Tultex, VF Imagewear, and later Nautica's Martinsville distribution center — was first appointed to Martinsville City Council in 1994, then elected in his own right in 1995 and again in 2000. He became Mayor in 2002 and held the seat continuously through 2018, making him, for most of that period, the longest-serving active member of council. Outside city government, he was active for years with the Collinsville Jaycees, serving as president, vice president, and chairman of the board.

In June 2018, Teague announced he would not seek reelection, ending 21 years on council, including 16 as Mayor.

"I am going to stay involved in the community. I've been born and raised here; lived here pretty much all of my life so I'll still be involved in other ways and I'm involved on other boards and commissions now, but I'll continue to do some of that stuff... in other angles."
— Gene Teague, announcing he would not seek reelection, June 2018

He framed the decision as making room for new voices rather than any dispute or controversy — "I've decided to give someone else a chance to be on the board" was his stated reasoning at the time.

Era Two
The Return
February – April 2026

Teague's name resurfaced in city governance months before his actual return, in an October 2024 council work session on charter reform. Discussing a proposed council term-limit rule, Vice Mayor Kathy Lawson — who would later serve alongside Teague during his 2026 appointment — cited him by name as her argument against the idea.

"The longer you are in, the more you learn. I think about [former mayor] Gene Teague. His experience as a longtime member of council was invaluable."
— Kathy Lawson, Martinsville City Council work session, October 2024

On February 23, 2026, Circuit Court Judge G. Carter Greer ordered Teague's appointment to the council seat vacated by Mayor LC Jones's suspension, acting under Virginia Code §24.2-236, which permits a court to appoint a "suitable person" to act in a suspended officer's place. Teague accepted the appointment and declined to accept payment for his time on council.

What The Record Shows

Teague occupied the seat for roughly two months, through the period when council twice considered releasing the Sands Anderson workplace investigation and continued routine budget work amid the broader crisis. No source reviewed documents Teague taking any contested position, casting any notable vote, or commenting publicly on the underlying allegations against Jones during this period — his presence reads, on the available record, as caretaking rather than active participation in the dispute itself.
