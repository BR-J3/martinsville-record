Person Record · Full Career

Aaron Rawls

Martinsville City Council Member — 2022–Present

Physically removed from chambers for opposing a raise that had already been secretly signed. Three documented confrontations over the following year. The sworn affidavit he signed the day before the removal petition was filed is the single most detailed piece of evidence in this entire record. He is now a named defendant in two federal lawsuits filed by the people his affidavit describes.

The Ejection — 2025 The Affidavit — 2026 Aftermath / Federal Defendant — 2026
Era One
The Ejection
March 25, 2025

Full detail in Chapter 3. On March 25, 2025, Rawls spoke against a proposed pay raise for City Manager Aretha Ferrell-Benavides, not knowing it had already been signed by Jones alone seven days earlier. Jones told him to "fall in order"; Rawls said he didn't understand the instruction. Deputy Sheriff Reva Keen removed him from chambers. Rawls learned the raise had already been signed via news media roughly six weeks later, on or about April 30, 2025.

What The Record Shows

Rawls's own later words on this moment, from the March 27, 2025 press conference: "Notice that no one thought about removing me until I started criticizing pay raises for executives. That was the thing that got me removed."

He filed federal complaint 4:25-cv-00028 on June 2, 2025, naming Keen, Ferrell-Benavides, and Jones, alleging First Amendment violation and retaliation for protected speech.

Era Two
The Affidavit
Notarized January 12, 2026

Rawls's notarized affidavit — signed one day before the CL26-011 removal petition was filed — is the single most detailed sworn document in this entire record. It is organized into four sections, summarized here. This is Rawls's sworn testimony. It has not been independently adjudicated by any court.

Section 1 — November 12, 2025 Closed Session

Four council members (Gravely, Lawson, Mei, Rawls) convened with Sands Anderson and Woods Rogers attorneys for a closed session on the forensic audit's preliminary findings and Ferrell-Benavides's EEOC complaint. Per the affidavit, "previous disclosures by city employees and examination of city records indicated what a reasonable person may discern to be evidence that Ferrell-Benavides engaged in activity in violation of Virginia's Criminal Code."

Jones entered the session at approximately 6:45 PM despite being told not to, and refused repeated recusal requests from Sands Anderson attorney Steve Durbin — causing the Woods Rogers attorneys to decline to give EEOC counsel in his presence. The affidavit also flags that Paul Goldman, who corresponded with media and council on Jones's and Ferrell-Benavides's behalf, could not be confirmed as a licensed Virginia attorney.

Section 2 — Financial Misconduct and Retaliation Timeline

Key Dates, Per the Affidavit

Stanfield's April 13 disclosure documented: failure to comply with federal reporting requirements; deficiencies mirroring the Duncanville, TX audit; appropriation of city funds without council authorization; and a lack of credit card controls — 134 city employees held credit cards, with expenses frequently lacking receipts or business justification.

What The Record Shows

Investigators on both the workplace investigation and the Brown Edwards forensic audit cited Jones's interference as "a detriment to their work" — the investigators' own characterization, not Rawls's.

Section 3 — Threats and Intimidation

Sworn Testimony — Not Adjudicated

March 25, 2025, Room 208, prior to the council session: per Rawls, Jones shook the back of his chair forcefully for several seconds, then threw an unoccupied chair. A witness reportedly told Rawls what he observed "was assault."

A contradicting account exists. Deputy Reva Keen's own sworn federal court declaration (July 23, 2025) states that HR Director Travis Hodge told her, before that same meeting, that Rawls had thrown a chair — not Jones. This record cannot resolve which account is accurate; both are sworn-adjacent statements relayed through different people, and are presented here side by side. Full detail on Keen's declaration: her own page.

May 13, 2025, public meeting: per the same affidavit, Jones aggressively unseated himself and approached Rawls. Councilman Rayshaun Gravely intercepted him. Armed police approached the dais. Council recessed.

January 8, 2026, public meeting: Jones again approached a council member — this time Julian Mei. Law enforcement intervened. Mei, who has a documented panic disorder he has publicly addressed, required assistance leaving chambers.

Section 4 — The March 17 Closed Session

Notice for the "special called closed session" was sent at 9:32 PM the Thursday before — after the working day ended. Per the affidavit, this violated the three-working-day notice requirement, and Sands Anderson was not present or notified as required under § 15.2-1418. The next day, Jones unilaterally signed an Employee Action/Change Form granting Ferrell-Benavides a $34,145 raise plus a city take-home vehicle.

What The Record Shows

Both Jones and Ferrell-Benavides made public remarks between March 18 and April 18, 2025 suggesting no raise had been discussed or awarded. CFO Richard Stanfield confirmed to Rawls on April 18 that the increase had in fact been processed March 18, effective April 1.

Era Three
Aftermath / Federal Defendant
2026 – Present

CL26-011 was dismissed with prejudice May 15, 2026. Krudys, Jones's attorney, publicly attributed the recall effort to Rawls in court and to the press that same day.

On June 9, 2026 — 25 days after the dismissal — Rawls was named a defendant in two separate federal suits: Jones v. Rawls and Sands Anderson, and Ferrell-Benavides v. City of Martinsville, Rawls, and Sands Anderson.

Contested Claim — Both Sides Shown

Jones's and Ferrell-Benavides's claim: the investigation was retaliatory, sparked by questions over Sands Anderson's 245% fee increase, with findings "manufactured" and fed to the press and prosecutors.

Rawls's direct, on-the-record rebuttal: "Sands Anderson manufactured nothing. They received disclosures from employees, including the chief financial officer of the city who observed this wrongdoing." This points back to Stanfield's whistleblower letter — documented in Chapter 1 and in Section 2 above — as the actual origin.

The "White Vote" Claim — A 2024 Origin

Jones's 2026 lawsuit alleges Rawls vowed to block Rayshaun Gravely from receiving "any white vote" in the city, tied to a conversation about Gravely's legal history before the 2024 council race. This is not solely a 2026 litigation claim — it traces to a contemporaneous Henry County Enterprise article from August 23, 2024, in which both men are quoted on the record. Full detail, including Rawls's denial and his characterization of Gravely's qualifications, is documented on Gravely's page.

Rawls's 2024 Denial, On The Record

Rawls's stated position (Aug 2024): Jones "tried to make it a race thing to cover up his agenda because he can't support it with substance." Rawls said his actual objection was that Gravely has "zero translatable skills or experience to this job," and separately stated Gravely "has been arrested three times for hurting women" — a count not independently confirmed elsewhere in this record (see Gravely's page for the reconciled timeline).

The FOIA Documents and the 150 Pages

Rawls posted FOIA documents publicly on his Google account at the time of their release, including signatures and other identifying information. Jennifer Bowles has separately stated, on the record, that 150 pages were sent by Rawls and "the gentleman Eric — I think his last name was Flynn" (confirmed elsewhere in this record as Eric Payne) to attorneys, and that those pages were "entered into Claude AI" during the case.

Contested Claim — Both Sides Shown

Bowles's claim: "It's not a question about AI. It's the fact Aaron said he didn't send the pages. Melody said she asked Eric Payne if he had anything to do with it. He said no. So it was untruthful."

No direct, on-the-record statement from Rawls disputing this specific claim has been located. The documents themselves are confirmed in hand; whether Rawls personally denied sending them, as Bowles alleges, is not independently confirmed here. Separately, a April 23, 2026 WDBJ7 report quotes Rawls describing special prosecutor Harrison as having received "150 pages of additional evidence" shortly before a possible non-suit — confirming the 150 pages are real case evidence, independent of the dispute over who sent them or how they were compiled.

Open Question

Were the 150 pages privileged city council information, as Bowles raised? Not yet resolved in the public record.
